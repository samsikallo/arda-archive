# THE PEOPLES OF ARDA — A DEMOGRAPHIC MODEL
### Races, subraces, populations and armies across the Ages, built solely from the corpus in /home/raz/samsi

**Source hierarchy.** Primary: Tolkien's narrative texts (Silmarillion; LotR + Appendices; Hobbit; Unfinished Tales; the three Great Tales; Fall of Númenor; Nature of Middle-earth [NoME]; History of Middle-earth I–XII [HoME]). Secondary within the corpus (flagged wherever used): Fonstad's Atlas, Hammond & Scull's Reader's Companion, the two Guides. *The Letters of J.R.R. Tolkien* is named in the task but is **not present in the folder**; no Letters citations are used. **No external sources were used anywhere in this model** — the "External information" heading required by the brief is therefore empty by design, and stated as such at the end.

**Labeling convention used throughout:**
- **[CANON]** — directly stated in the corpus (quotation + source given).
- **[INFERRED — H/M/L]** — reasoned estimate from canon data; method shown; confidence High/Medium/Low.
- **[SPECULATIVE — L]** — flagged, minimized, never load-bearing.
- Where the corpus is silent, the model says exactly: *"The attached documents do not provide enough information to answer this."*

## 1. TAXONOMY OF THE SPEAKING PEOPLES

### 1.1 Prose overview

**The Ainur** stand outside biology: the Valar (fourteen after Melkor's fall) and their lesser kin the Maiar (uncounted; the five Istari, the Balrogs "3 or at most 7", Sauron, Melian — who uniquely entered an elvish marriage and bore Lúthien) neither breed nor die, only diminish or depart.

**Elves (Quendi)** divide at the Great March: the **Avari** (~⅓, stayed east — never counted again in the corpus); the **Eldar** of the three kindreds — **Vanyar** (smallest kindred, wholly in Aman after the March), **Noldor** (craft-masters; nine-tenths went into exile to Beleriand, one tithe refused), and the great **Teleri** branch, splitting into the Aman Teleri, the **Sindar** of Beleriand (Doriath's Iathrim, the coastal **Falathrim**, the northern Mithrim), the **Nandor** who left the March at Anduin (→ **Green-elves of Ossiriand** and the **Silvan Elves** of Greenwood and Lórien). Elvish demography is the inverse of mortal: immortal, slow-maturing (adult ~50–100), early-marrying, few children (≤4, usually 1–2, all born in a brief window), and *fertility effectively ceases with age and with grief*; population declines only by sword and by sailing West — but rarely recovers, because the begetting-will does not return. Hence the Eldar of Middle-earth are a wasting asset from the Nirnaeth onward: "the number grew less as ages passed."

**Men (Atani)** are the counterweight: short-lived, fast-breeding, endlessly replaceable and endlessly migrating. The corpus distinguishes the **Edain** (Three Houses: Bëor's — dark-haired, slighter, entered ~2,000 grown men strong; **Haladin/People of Haleth** — separate tongue, stockade-dwelling, repeatedly "dwindled... by war to a small people"; **House of Hador** — tall, golden-haired, most numerous), their apotheosis the **Númenóreans/Dúnedain** (thrice-lengthened lives, late marriage, small families — a high-civilization, low-fertility profile Tolkien states explicitly and dooms twice, at the Downfall and again in Gondor's "childless kings"), and the **Middle Men** of ordinary demography (**Northmen/Éothéod → Rohirrim**, Dale-men, Beornings, Woodmen, Bree-men, Dunlendings, the White-Mountain remnant → Dead of Dunharrow), plus the **Men of Darkness** — Easterlings (Ulfings/Bórrim in the First Age; Wainriders, Balchoth, Rhûn confederacies later), **Haradrim** (Near/Far), **Variags of Khand**, **Corsairs of Umbar** (Black-Númenórean-led) — for whom the corpus supplies armies but never censuses — and the archaic relict **Drúedain/Woses** (small, mound-dwelling watchers, "always few," dwindling in every age they appear).

**Dwarves (Khazâd)**: seven houses from seven Fathers — in the west the **Longbeards/Durin's Folk** (Gundabad-wakened; Khazad-dûm → wandering → Erebor/Iron Hills/Ered Luin), **Firebeards and Broadbeams** (Nogrod and Belegost in Ered Luin — Beleriand's armorers), and four eastern houses (Ironfists, Stiffbeards, Blacklocks, Stonefoots) known only by name and direction. Their demography is the corpus's most explicit: ~⅓ female, under ⅓ of males ever marrying, single lifelong marriages, ~250-year lives — a people that "increases slowly" and can only shrink under persecution.

**Hobbits**: an offshoot of Men (Prologue) in three kindreds — **Harfoots** (most numerous), **Stoors** (riverine; the Gladden remnant produced Sméagol), **Fallohides** (fewest; the leader-families) — merged in the Shire (founded T.A. 1601) plus Bree. Large families, dense genealogies, ~100-year lives: the highest sustained fertility profile in the corpus.

**Ents** (Onodrim): eldest of speaking peoples, sexed (Ents/Entwives) but sundered — the Entwives' gardens were destroyed in the Second Age and with them all reproduction; the march of ~50+ Ents to Isengard is effectively a census of the remnant. **Terminal decline.** [CANON]

**Orcs**: Morgoth's corrupted breed, multiplying "after the manner of the Children" but invisible to the corpus outside war; **Trolls** likewise counterfeit and countless-but-uncounted; **the Great Eagles, Dragons, Wargs, Shelob's brood**: sentient or semi-sentient, no demographic data. [CANON gaps noted]

## 2. CHRONOLOGY AND METHODOLOGY

### 2.1 The time-frame used
Years of the Lamps (no countable peoples yet) → **Years of the Trees** (Elves awake at Cuiviénen; Great March; Exile of the Noldor) → **First Age of the Sun** (F.A. 1–590: the Beleriand wars — Bragollach 455, Nirnaeth 472, falls of Nargothrond 495 / Doriath c. 502 / Gondolin 510; War of Wrath 545–587 era, Beleriand drowned) → **Second Age** (S.A. 1–3441: Númenor founded 32; Eregion destroyed 1697; Númenor's curve 5k→15M; Downfall 3319; Last Alliance 3429–3441) → **Third Age** (T.A. 1–3021: Plague 1636; Angmar wars to 1975; Rohirrim settle 2510; dragon/orc wars 2770–2799; War of the Ring 3018–19) → **Fourth Age** (dominion of Men). Tree-year conversions differ across the corpus (1 V.Y. = 9.582 or 144 sun-years); the model plots eras as segments, not one continuous absolute axis, to avoid manufacturing precision. [CANON chronology; method note]

### 2.2 Demographic methods applied (each estimate names which it uses)
- **M1 Warrior-ratio.** A full emergency muster of an agrarian people ≈ 20–25% of total population (all able-bodied males); a sustainable standing field-force ≈ 5–10%. Anchors: Rohan's Full Muster "not less than 120 [per éored] ... one hundredth part" = **12,000+ Riders** vs Théoden's actual 6,000 (UT, Battles of the Fords of Isen n.36); Gondor's outland levies described as a **tithe** of their strength (LotR V.1, Lossarnach "two hundreds... we hoped for ten times").
- **M2 Named-fleet / named-host arithmetic.** Direct canon counts (2,000 Bëorian men; Turgon's 10,000; Dáin's 500; nine ships of the Faithful; 150–300 ships of Elros...) scaled by household multipliers (1 grown man ≈ 4–5 total population in mortal agrarian societies; ≈ 2.5–3 for Eldar given small completed families).
- **M3 Tolkien's own figures.** Wherever NoME gives numbers (Elvish increase tables; Númenor's 5–10k → 200–350k → 2M → 15M) they are used as **[CANON]** rails.
- **M4 Catastrophe shocks.** Canon fractions: Thranduil brought home "barely a third" from Dagorlad; "three parts of the goblin warriors of the North perished" (Five Armies); "Barely half" survived Azanulbizar; Esgaroth lost ~¼ ("three quarters... escaped alive"); Númenor: total loss less colonies.
- **M5 Carrying-capacity sanity checks** from the geographic reconstruction (areas: Shire ~18–21k sq mi; Númenor ~180k sq mi [CANON]; Rohan, Gondor, Beleriand extents) at pre-modern densities of 1–40 people/sq mi depending on land quality.
- **M6 Sex-splitting.** Baseline 50:50 at birth for Elves (canon 72+72), Men, Hobbits; Dwarves fixed at ~67:33 [CANON]; war shocks debit adult males (armies are male in the corpus, with flagged exceptions); sieges' "women and children" evacuations used as qualitative confirmation.

- Male/female view-switch: total | split (♂ solid, ♀ dashed overlay) using the sex-share layer.
- Hover tooltips: value, range, confidence badge, method codes, and the governing citation.
- Confidence encoded visually: line opacity (H solid → L faint) + dotted segments where the model interpolates between attested points.

## 6. CONFLICTS, GAPS AND EXTERNAL INFORMATION

- **Internal conflicts handled:** two Valian-year lengths (9.582 vs 144 sun-years) — avoided by era-segmenting; NoME's ~30,000-Eldar March vs the implied scale of Beleriand hosts (Turgon's 10,000 alone) — reconciled by centuries of Aman/Doriath growth between March and Exile, and flagged M/L; old Silmarillion host-rhetoric ("countless") never converted to numbers.
- **Systematic gaps (stated, not filled):** Avari totals; Easterling/Haradrim/Khand populations; orc reproduction and female orcs; troll/dragon/eagle counts; Maiar total; Shire census.
- **External information: none used.** Every figure above derives from the corpus in /home/raz/samsi; Fonstad's Atlas (a corpus member, but secondary) contributed only cross-checks on battle strengths, each marked where used in §3.

*Prepared as a reasoned model, not a revelation: wherever Tolkien gave a number it rules; wherever he gave none, the estimate is labeled, justified, and dispensable.*
