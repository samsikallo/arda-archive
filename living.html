The Living Things of Arda

A herbarium and a bestiary — the plants, the kinds of creature, and the beasts that bear a name

Tolkien named his world down to its flowers. Some he described at length — the mallorn that does not shed its leaves until spring, the athelas whose virtue wakes only in a king's hand. Others he named once and never described at all, and that silence is recorded here rather than filled in: four fragrant trees of Númenor share a single clause between them, and no fish of the Forest River is ever named.

Where he changed his mind, both minds are shown. The origin of the Orcs has four incompatible answers across the Silmarillion and Morgoth's Ring and none is asserted; alfirin is three different flowers depending on the page; the Ents' First Age role is flagged as Christopher's editorial reconstruction. The pale flowers of Lothlórien are described in detail and never named — and the corpus never calls them niphredil, whatever everyone assumes.
