The Devices⚒ The Forge

Tolkien devised a true heraldic grammar for the Elves, distinct from the arms of Men and Dwarves. Its rules — set out in his own notes and drawings (Hammond & Scull, J.R.R. Tolkien: Artist & Illustrator; Heraldry of Middle-earth):

  • Shape tells kind. A circle bears a woman's device; a lozenge a man's; a square a house or an impersonal thing.
  • Points tell rank. The number of points about the rim rises with dignity — about four for a prince, six to eight for a king, and as many as sixteen for a great ancestor of a house.
  • Symmetry and jewelled colour. Elvish devices are radially symmetric, wrought like enamel or gem-work, each charge repeated in rotation about a centre.
  • Men and Dwarves differ. The Númenórean-descended realms bear pictorial charges on a field (Gondor's White Tree, Rohan's horse); the Enemy's marks are stark and singular (the Red Eye, the White Hand).

The rule in Tolkien's own hand. Set down beside the devices he drew in 1960:

Women within a circle personal / Men within a lozenge / general (impersonal) designs or emblems of a family square (or [?] once, circular). The rank was usually held to be shown by number of ‘points’ which reached the outer rim — four was prince) 6–8 kings / the great ancestors sometimes had as many [as] 16 as in House of Finwë. — quoted in Hammond & Scull, J.R.R. Tolkien: Artist & Illustrator, p. 191

What Tolkien actually drew. Twenty-eight finished devices survive from 1960–61: five of Idril, three of Eärendil, two of Finwë, and one each for Finarfin, Gil-galad, Lúthien, Fëanor, Fingolfin, Finrod, Elwë, Melian, Bëor, Hador, Beren, Haleth and the Silmarils. They are not shields but kaleidoscopic mandalas — geometric for the men, floral for the women — closer to Japanese mon than to European arms.

The Forge of Devices. Wright a device by the rules above: the shape tells the bearer's kind, the rim-points tell rank, and within the field you may set a central charge, an attendant charge turning about it, concentric rings, a petal-ring of petals, lens-petals or rays, and jewels set about — the encrusted, mandala-like manner of the devices Tolkien drew. Sixteen tinctures. Begin from any of the reconstructed devices and alter it, or take a device at random. The forge reads back a blazon and warns when the points fall outside the ranks Tolkien names. Everything the forge draws is new and original — computed here, copied from nothing.
