The Registers

Five registers built from Tolkien's own words — every entry anchored to a passage that was found, not remembered

These five registers were built by searching the corpus itself, under one rule: nothing is written down that cannot be shown. Every entry carries a verbatim quotation located in a tier 1 or tier 2 source. Where Tolkien contradicted himself, the conflict is recorded rather than resolved; where a thing is named but never described, that absence is recorded too.

The discipline refused four things that are widely believed and are not in Tolkien: “Mairon” as Sauron's name (it is in Parma Eldalamberon 17, which this archive does not hold), “You shall not pass” (he says You cannot pass), “Samwise the Brave” (the text has Samwise the stouthearted), and Brego as Théoden's horse (Brego is the second King of the Mark). Each absence is noted in place.
