## 1. THE CANONICAL TREES REPRODUCED
Tolkien's own drawn/tabulated trees in the corpus, all incorporated: **Silmarillion tables I–V** (House of Finwë & the Noldorin descent of Elrond and Elros; Descendants of Olwë and Elwë; House of Bëor; Houses of Hador & the People of Haleth; the Sundering of the Elves); **App A** lines (Elros' Kings of Númenor; Lords of Andúnië → Elendil; Kings of Arnor/Arthedain and Chieftains; Kings of Gondor and Stewards; the Line of Eorl; Durin's Folk); **App C** hobbit trees (Baggins, Took, Brandybuck, Gamgee/Longfather-tree); the Great-Tales volumes' identical tables.

## 2. THE GREAT MERGE — one graph where canon connects [all C]
The corpus yields **one vast connected component** spanning Ainur, Elves and Men, stitched by these canonical marriages: Finwë ↔ Indis (Noldor–Vanyar); Finarfin ↔ Eärwen (Noldor–Teleri of Alqualondë, joining Olwë's line); **Thingol ↔ Melian** (Elves–Ainur); **Beren ↔ Lúthien** (Edain–Doriath); Húrin ↔ Morwen and Huor ↔ Rían (Hador–Bëor); Galdor ↔ Hareth and Haldir ↔ Glóredhel (Hador–Haleth double bond, Silm 17 table IV/V); **Tuor ↔ Idril** (Hador–Gondolin); **Eärendil ↔ Elwing** (uniting all three Elf-Man lines with Maiar blood); Elros → the whole Númenórean royal line → (Silmariën) → Lords of Andúnië → **Elendil → Isildur/Anárion → both kingdoms → Aragorn**; Elrond ↔ Celebrían (joining Galadriel & Celeborn's line); **Aragorn ↔ Arwen** (reuniting the sundered Half-elven branches, App A I(v)); Imrahil's line ↔ Éomer (Lothíriel) and **Faramir ↔ Éowyn** (Gondor–Rohan–Dol Amroth); Thengel ↔ Morwen of Lossarnach (Rohan–Gondor).
**Two components remain genuinely separate** — Durin's Folk and the Hobbit families (internally richly connected: Baggins–Took via Belladonna, Baggins–Brandybuck via Primula, Took–Brandybuck via Mirabella/Esmeralda, Gamgee→Fairbairn via Elanor ↔ Fastred). No canon kinship joins Dwarves or Hobbits to the great tree: stating the separation is fidelity, not failure. Sméagol's kin: only an unnamed matriarch grandmother [C, LotR I.2] — drawn as an unnamed node.
**Fosterage edges [C]:** Túrin fostered by Thingol (CoH); Aragorn fostered by Elrond (App A); Frodo adopted as Bilbo's heir (LotR I.1). Tuor fostered by Annael the Grey-elf (UT). **House-affiliation edge:** Círdan "kin of Olwë and Elwë" (HoME XII Last Writings — M, no parent named).

## 3. DOCUMENTED CONFLICTS [each kept visible in the data]
- **Gil-galad's father:** published Silmarillion = son of Fingon; HoME XII (*Shibboleth*) = Tolkien's late decision: son of **Orodreth** (himself moved to son of Angrod). The graph shows the Orodreth link (late intent) dashed-M with both citations; the Fingon variant noted in the panel. 
- **Orodreth's placement** (brother vs nephew of Finrod) — same source pair, same handling.
- **Amrod's death at Losgar** (Shibboleth) vs surviving to the Third Kinslaying (Silm) — noted on his panel.
- Celeborn's ancestry (Sindar, grandson of Elmo [UT] vs Teleri of Aman [late]) — UT reading shown, M.

## 4. CONDENSATION RULE [stated, not hidden]
Long single-file royal chains are drawn with **ellipsis nodes** ("⟨17 kings, App A list⟩") between anchors (e.g. Elros → Silmariën/Tar-Minastir → Tar-Palantír/Míriel/Ar-Pharazôn; Isildur → ⟨chieftains⟩ → Arathorn II; Meneldil → ⟨kings⟩ → Eärnur; Mardil → ⟨stewards⟩ → Ecthelion II; Eorl → ⟨kings⟩ → Thengel; Durin I → ⟨the line⟩ → Thrór). Every omitted name is in the cited App A tables; nothing is lost, only visually folded. Full-name expansion is a data question, not a canon question.

## 5. HERALDRY REGISTER [canon devices only; no invented blazons]
Star of the House of Fëanor (eight-rayed silver — Doors of Durin, LotR II.4); emblems of Durin (crown, anvil & hammer, seven stars — ibid.); Elendil/Gondor: White Tree, Seven Stars and Crown on sable (LotR V; "Seven stars and seven stones and one white tree"); the Stewards: white banner without device (LotR V.1); Dol Amroth: silver Swan-ship on blue (LotR V.1/6); Rohan: White Horse upon green (LotR III/V); Fingolfin's blue-and-silver banners (Silm 13); Númenor: no single royal device is described — "The attached documents do not provide enough information to answer this."

## 6. NAME MEANINGS
Provided in the data only where the corpus glosses them (chiefly the Silmarillion's Appendix of name-elements and in-text translations): e.g. Fëanor "Spirit of Fire", Tinúviel "Nightingale", Elrond/Elros ("star-dome"/"star-foam" elements el+rond/ros), Eärendil "lover of the sea", Elwing "star-spray", Elessar "Elfstone", Morwen (mor "dark" + wen "maiden"), Turambar "Master of Doom", Mormegil "Black Sword". Names without a corpus gloss carry none.

The graph now carries **397 figures / 489 relations**: every *named* ruler in the corpus is drawn — all 25 rulers of Númenor (UT *Line of Elros*, with spouses Erendis, Hallacar, Herucalmo/Tar-Anducal and the Vëantur–Almarian link), the Lords of Andúnië that the texts name (Valandil, Eärendur, Lindórië, Númendil, Amandil), Isildur's four sons (UT *Disaster of the Gladden Fields*), all Kings of Arnor and Arthedain and all 16 Chieftains, all Kings of Gondor with the Kin-strife web (Vidugavia→Vidumavi, Castamir and his Corsair great-grandsons Angamaitë & Sangahyando, Berúthiel from UT), all 26 Stewards (Húrin of Emyn Arnen → Denethor II, plus Barahir the chronicler via Elboron), the Dol Amroth legend-line (Imrazôr ↔ Mithrellas, UT, marked M as CT does), the full House of Eorl in three lines (Léod, Baldor, Helm's children, Hild→Fréaláf, the Poros twins), and Durin's line entire (Durin VI → Náin II named kings, Durin VII the Last).
