# THE TONGUES AND LETTERS OF ARDA

Sources actually present: LotR App E–F; Silmarillion Appendix of name-elements; The Etymologies & Lhammas (HoME V); Quendi and Eldar (HoME XI); The Shibboleth of Fëanor & Dangweth Pengoloð (HoME XII); Lowdham's Report on the Adunaic Language (HoME IX); NoME; UT & Great-Tales name-lists; BoLT I–II appendices (early Qenya/Gnomish lexicon extracts); Treason of Isengard appendix on runes. **Named in the brief but ABSENT from the folder and not used: Parma Eldalamberon, Vinyar Tengwar, The Road Goes Ever On, The Letters.** External information: none.

## 2. SCRIPTS
**Documented in this corpus:** Tengwar of Fëanor (App E: full table, témar/tyeller structure, tehtar, Quenya-mode & General-Use conventions; King's Letter specimen HoME IX; Ring-inscription; West-gate inscription II.4). Cirth/Angerthas (App E: full table with Angerthas Daeron vs Moria vs Erebor value-columns and the structural principles; Balin's tomb; ToI appendix shows the earlier LotR-draft runes). Sarati/Tengwar of Rúmil: NAMED (App E: "the oldest letters... of Rúmil"; Silm; NoME mentions) — **glyph tables are not in this corpus** ("The attached documents do not provide enough information" for writing in it). Valmaric, Andyoqenya, Qenyatic, Gondolinic runes: absent from the corpus entirely (they live in Parma Eldalamberon) — catalogued as external-named-only, nothing rendered. The Hobbit's dwarf-map runes = Anglo-Saxon futhorc used as a device (stated by Tolkien in the corpus prefatory note).
**Interactive rendering honesty:** the writing-lab glyphs are schematic constructions that follow App E's own structural system (stem + lúva composition for tengwar; stem + branch modifications for cirth: added stroke = voice, both-sides = nasal etc.) — the systematic logic is canon, the letterform details are calligraphic and vary in Tolkien's specimens; App E's plates remain the authority.

## 3. GRAMMAR — what is actually known (summary; full rules in JSON)
**Quenya [rich]:** phonology from App E (stress rule stated!; permitted finals: vowels, -n -l -r -s -t); plural -r/-i, dual -u/-t, partitive -li; cases attested in texts: gen. -o (Vardo, Oiolossëo), poss. -va (miruvóreva), allative -nna (Rómenna, Elendilenna), locative -ssë (Lóriendessë), ablative -llo (Rómello, sindanóriello), instrumental -nen (lírinen, súrinen); verb: aorist/present, past -në, perfect with augment (utúvienyes, avánier), future -uva (laituvalmet, enquantuva); pronominal suffixes -n(yë), -lmë, -ntë, -t...; all cited to the corpus texts they occur in. What is NOT here: a full paradigm table by Tolkien — flagged.
**Sindarin [rich but implicit]:** no grammar essay in corpus; rules shown by paradigm examples: i-affection plurals (adan/edain, amon/emyn, orod/ered), lenition after i 'the' sg. and articles (i-Cherdir? King's Letter: e-mbar, i-Varanduiniant...), stop-mutations in compounds, genitive by juxtaposition (Ennyn Durin Aran Moria), verb 3sg past -ant (teithant), imperatives -o (edro!, daro!, tiro!). Marked [C-example / I-rule] throughout.
**Adûnaic [formal]:** Lowdham's Report gives phonology (triconsonantal Semitic-style bases, e.g. K-R-B), noun classes (strong/weak), numbers incl. dual, cases: Normal, Subjective, Objective; genders m/f/common/neuter — the corpus's only complete formal grammar; adopted wholesale with §-cites.
**Khuzdul:** radical system noted in App F; aglâb vs iglishmêk (Q&E); structure Semitic-like [C], no paradigms — flagged insufficient.
**Black Speech/Westron/others:** word-lists + App F typology only; translation beyond glossing marked impossible-in-canon.

## 4. THE LHAMMAS TREE & CONFLICTS
Language family tree per Lhammas (HoME V) with CT's caveat that it predates the later external history; overlaid with the Q&E (HoME XI) revision: Quendian → Eldarin/Avarin; Eldarin → Common Eldarin → Quenya–Telerin branch vs Sindarin; Nandorin split on the March. Major documented development-conflicts kept visible: (1) "Noldorin"→Sindarin re-basing (Etym. vs post-LotR texts; CT's commentary in HoME V intro & XI); (2) early Qenya/Gnomish (BoLT lexicons) vs mature forms — early layer shown as a separate stratum, never merged; (3) Lhammas' Valarin-descent of all Elvish vs later independent origin (Q&E) — later adopted, both shown; (4) þ/s merger politics (Shibboleth).

## 5. TRANSLATOR — honest scope
Gloss-level translation only: English→Quenya/Sindarin picks attested lemmas, shows morphological assembly with each affix cited, and REFUSES silently-invented vocabulary: gaps yield "no attested word" (with nearest semantic neighbours). No syntax generation is claimed canon; assembled phrases are labeled [reconstruction] when any bound morphology is applied beyond attested collocations. Elvish→English segments and glosses from the lexicon. Khuzdul/Adûnaic/Black Speech: gloss-lookup only, fragmentary-data warning. Neo-forms: none included — the layer exists in the schema (flag "N") and ships empty by design.

## 6. REVISION 2 — the alphabets rebuilt, corrections, and External information
**Corpus re-verification (App E read verbatim this pass) fixed these errors in rev-1:** (a) tengwa 12 hwesta in the Westron/General-Use application is **ch**, not kh — App E's Grade-3 row reads "th, f, sh, ch"; (b) hyarmen = h (older value hy), not "hy" plain; (c) **halla** added — "a simple raised stem without bow", breath-h, and before r/l = voiceless hr/hl; (d) the a-tehta's quick form is the circumflex-like sign, and Quenya may omit a entirely (clm = calma); (e) the o/u curl assignment is per-document — Ring-inscription: right-open = u; title-page: right-open = o — both canon; (f) the West-gate (Beleriand) mode's Grade shift: 21 = n, 17 = nn; (g) nasal-bar above / doubling-bar below / s-hook / underposed dot / extended-stem 'the'/'of' abbreviations all documented from App E; (h) the Angerthas exception (№6 m, №5 hw), the s/h hesitation (34/35/54), Moria's value-shift and Erebor's 43-z/17-ks/57-58 all carried into the drawn table.
**The alphabets are now drawn, not schematic:** the site's Alphabets tab renders the full Fëanorian table (24-grid + 12 additional + halla + carriers), the full Angerthas 1–60 with Daeron/Moria/Erebor columns and the oldest-layer marked, and the Erebor ligatures — using traced vector letterforms from Wikimedia Commons, each checked against the App E plate in the corpus PDF. The Writing Lab writes five modes with these glyphs: classical Quenya, General Use, and cirth in Daeron/Moria/Erebor values.
**External information (authorized this revision; never overriding App E):** Wikipedia — Tengwar, Cirth, Sarati, Tolkien's scripts (structure summaries, the plate-transcription of Angerthas values — every value App E states in prose agrees with it); Tolkien Gateway / Eldalamberon.com (publication histories: Sarati = PE 13; Valmaric + Early Qenya = PE 14; Gondolinic runes in 'Early Runic Documents' = PE 15; pre-Fëanorian family incl. Falassin/Noriac/Banyaric/Sinyatic); Amanyë Tenceli (Sarati structure: vowel-diacritics turned toward their sarat, mirrored right-side, bar carriers); Tecendil & Glaemscribe (living mode-conventions; Glaemscribe's cited sources incl. PE 20 and Quettar 13 for numerals); Wikimedia Commons (vector glyph files, user-traced from Tolkien's published tables). The tengwar duodecimal numeral system is documented only externally (Quettar) and is described, not claimed as corpus canon. Sarati/Valmaric/Gondolinic/Andyoqenya/Qenyatic letterform tables remain unpublished in machine-readable form — their structures are documented [EXT] and honestly not rendered.

## 7. REVISION 3 — the deep mining and the Name-craft rework
**The corpus re-checked, and it paid:** two whole lexical strata were extracted entire this pass — (1) the **Silmarillion's Appendix of name-elements, complete: 191 entries** [C] (previous revisions had used it piecemeal), and (2) **all 467 root-articles of the Etymologies** (HoME V), machine-parsed with their Quenya/Noldorin derivative forms [C]. With the 240-entry core, the searchable lexicon now spans **~900 cited items**, and the Etymologies layer quietly answers things the old build lacked — including the **Eldarin numerals 1–10** (MINI through KAYAN, with the sixes-and-twelves counting habit of App D).
**New interactive features:** the **Name-craft tab** — an *analyzer* that unweaves any Tolkien name into corpus-glossed elements (Galadriel → galad 'radiance' + riel 'garlanded maiden', exactly as the Appendix has it) with unmatched parts declared as honest residue, never invented; a *forge* that composes new names from canon elements, every product stamped **NEO — not canon**; the complete **affix & word-parts table** (-rim, -ath, -ion, i-affection…); the **numerals**; the full element-glossary browser. Elsewhere: a 18-entry **Phrasebook** of every attested saying (Texts tab); App E's complete **pronunciation rulebook** (Grammar tab); the unified site chrome with cross-links; and lexicon search now merges all three layers.
**External information:** a *Further libraries* card (Eldamo, Parf Edhellen, Ardalambion, the transcribers) — pointers for going beyond the corpus, clearly [EXT]; no external form entered the data. The mistake-check found and fixed one parse-artifact (a 'connexion' fragment mis-lemmatized) and three appendix-internal lemmas restored (gal, galad, riel, galadh).
