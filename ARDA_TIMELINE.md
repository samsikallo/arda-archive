### The chronology behind the interactive time-map. Corpus-only; companion to RECONSTRUCTION.md (geography) and the arda_ref map (style/base).

**Task-1 note (the enhanced static map).** The corpus contains no tool for faithfully upscaling the raster `downloaded_map.jpeg` without inventing pixel detail; the project instead **redrew it as a vector plate in its own style** (`arda_ref.svg/png`, 12,000 px), preserving its composition, palette, black chain-work, green forest masses and red annal layer, while adding ~120 additional canon labels (roads, beacons, passes, the Girdle, Baynes-list names) and applying the documented corrections: Thangorodrim at its thrice-attested 150 leagues from Menegroth (Silm 10 vs the compressed map tradition — HoME XI's own discussion); the reference's uncanonical 506/538 annal dates dropped; Glanduin/Swanfleet labeled per Tolkien's 1969 correction (UT App. D; RC); dashed "unrecorded coast" honesty-markers; latitude registration per the 1967 letter quoted in RC. ## 1. GEOGRAPHIC CHANGE CHRONOLOGY

**Years of the Lamps.** Symmetric primeval world: lamps Illuin (N) and Ormal (S); one continuous landmass, seas in two great troughs; Valar dwell at Almaren amid the Great Lake. **[C — Silm 1; HoME IV Ambarkanta]** Fall of the Lamps: "the shape of Arda and the symmetry of its waters and its lands was marred" — inland seas Helcar (N) and Ringil (S) formed at the pillars' roots. **[C]** Shapes: schematic only **[I-L]**.

**Years of the Trees.** Battle of the Powers (Utumno destroyed): "the Great Sea... grew wide and deep"; the **Great Gulf** opens southward; **Bay of Balar** formed; the new highlands of Dorthonion and Hithlum's fences raised; Beleriand's river-web takes its lasting shape. **[C — Silm 3]** Cuiviénen a bay of Helcar in the east **[C]**; its later identity with the shrunken Sea of Rhûn **[I-M — CT's query, HoME XI; NoME March-notes]**.

**First Age.** F.A. 455 (Bragollach): Ard-galen burned into **Anfauglith** — permanent desertification of the northern plain **[C — Silm 18]**. Tol Sirion defiled; Dorthonion becomes Taur-nu-Fuin ("Forest under Nightshade") — ecological corruption, not relief change **[C]**. F.A. 495–510: sacks of Nargothrond, Doriath, Gondolin — urban ruin, land intact **[C]**. **War of Wrath (c. 545–587): Beleriand broken and drowned** — "northward and westward many lands sank... in Ossiriand the walls of Ered Luin were broken, and a great gap was made... towards the south" → the **Gulf of Lhûn**; survivors' land = **Lindon** (Ossiriand's rim); remnant isles **Himling** (Himring's crown) and **Tol Fuin** (Taur-nu-Fuin's height); **Tol Morwen** preserves Túrin's stone at sea. **[C — Silm 24/RoP; C App A; UT Intro; RC]** Exact drowned-coast course: **[I-M]**, drawn as the wash on the base map.

**Second Age.** S.A. 32: **Númenor** raised by Ossë for the Edain — star-isle, 250-mi core, ~180,000 sq mi **[C — Akallabêth; UT Description; NoME]** (off this map's canvas; represented by badge). c. 600–1700: Númenórean harbour-works — **Vinyalondë/Lond Daer** at Gwathló-mouth **[C — UT Aldarion]**; the **great deforestation of Minhiriath and Enedwaith** ("devastation... incalculable"; natives flee to **Eryn Vorn** and the Dunland hills; after 1701 the Gwathló ran through treeless desert on both banks) **[C — UT App. D]** — the map's emptiest quarter is man-made. **Tharbad** dyked, bridged, garrisoned **[C]**. c. 1000: Sauron chooses **Mordor**; Barad-dûr rises (finished c. 1600) **[C — App B]**. 1693–1701: war wrecks **Eregion** (ruined evermore); Moria's gates shut **[C]**. 2280 Umbar made a great fortress; 2350 **Pelargir** built **[C — App B]**. **3319 the Downfall**: Númenor swallowed; **the world made round**; "all the coasts and seaward regions of the western world suffered great change"; Anduin's mouths shift — "the Bay of Belfalas was much filled at the east and south, so that Pelargir... was left far inland, and Anduin carved a new path by many mouths... Tolfalas was almost destroyed" **[C — Akallabêth; unique ToY note, HoME XII]**. 3320: realms-in-exile founded; 3429–41 war: **Dagorlad** made a memorial waste; the **Dead Marshes** later "crept" over the graves ("always creeping", III/IV) **[C]**.

**Third Age.** c. 2–3: Isildur plants the White Tree; Gladden disaster **[C]**. c. 1050→: shadow in Greenwood → **Mirkwood**; Dol Guldur rises **[C — App A/B]**. c. 1300s: **Angmar** organizes the Witch-realm at the Mountains' head **[C]**. 861 Arnor divided (see §2). 1409/1636/1974: wars and the **Great Plague** empty Eriador and Cardolan; Osgiliath depopulated (Dome of Stars burned 1437; city ruinous by 2475) **[C]**. **1856–1944**: east of Anduin lost; Wainrider wars **[C]**. **2510**: Calenardhon granted → **Rohan** (Isengard retained by Gondor as an enclave) **[C — UT Cirion]**. 2758–9 Long Winter; **2770 Smaug**: the **Desolation of Smaug** made around Erebor/Dale (healed after 2941: "the desolation was now filled with birds and blossoms... in autumn", Hobbit XIX) **[C]**. 2912: floods ruin **Tharbad**; Enedwaith/Minhiriath devastated again **[C — App B]**. 2941–51: Erebor refounded; Mordor re-occupied; Barad-dûr rebuilt **[C]**. Northmen's tree-felling cut Mirkwood's **East Bight** (long prior; "great indentation... made largely by tree-felling" — UT) **[C]**. **3019**: Isengard's ring un-made into the **Treegarth** (gardened, flooded, Watchwood); Helm's **Death Down** raised; **Barad-dûr, the Morannon and the Towers fall in earthquake and eruption**; Núrn freed to slaves; Dol Guldur's pits laid bare by Galadriel; **Eryn Lasgalen** proclaimed **[C — VI; App B]**.
**Early Fourth Age.** Minas Ithil (Morgul) "utterly destroyed" per decree **[C — VI.5]**; Ithilien re-gardened (Legolas's folk); the mallorn in the Party Field — "the only mallorn west of the Mountains and east of the Sea"; Westmarch added to the Shire (S.R. 1452 = F.A. ~31); Arnor's cities re-founded by Elessar (Annúminas restored) **[C — App A/B]**. Aman & Eressëa: removed from the circles of the world at the Downfall — reachable only by the Straight Road **[C]**.

## 2. POLITICAL BORDERS THROUGH TIME (register; polygons in arda_timemap.json)

Confidence: **H** = border named on rivers/ranges by Tolkien; **M** = "between X and Y" prose; **L** = schematic region.

**First Age Beleriand [Silm 14 is the charter]:** Hithlum (Fingolfin/Fingon; walls = Ered Wethrin/Ered Lómin — H); Nevrast (Turgon till c. 126 — H bounds); Gondolin (Echoriath ring — H); Dorthonion (Angrod/Aegnor, 60 leagues W–E — H; lost 455); Tol Sirion (Orodreth; lost 457); Doriath (Girdle of Melian: Neldoreth+Region+Nivrim — H); Nargothrond ("between Sirion and the sea... to Nenning", overlord "between Sirion and Teiglin" — H/M); Falas (Círdan: Brithombar/Eglarest — H; to 473); Brethil (Haladin, "between Teiglin and Sirion" — H); the March of Maedhros (Himring–Aglon–Gap — H) with Himlad (Celegorm/Curufin — H), Thargelion ("Gelion↔mountains, Rerir↔Ascar", Caranthir — H; lost 455), Estolad (M); Ossiriand (Green-elves: Ascar↔Adurant↔Gelion↔Ered Luin — H); Angband's dominion (the North; after 455 all north of the Andram effectively — M "Dor-deloth... borders ever increased southward", HoME V).
**Second Age:** Lindon (Gil-galad; Lune basin+coast — M); Eregion (750–1697; Glanduin south-bound — H per UT App. D); Khazad-dûm realm (east-gate vales — M); Silvan realms (Greenwood-whole under Oropher — M; Lórinand — M); Númenórean coastal lordships (Lond Daer, Umbar 2280, Pelargir 2350 — points+coastal strips, M/L); **Mordor** (the fenced land — H walls) from c. 1000; Sauron's wide empire "east and south" — L schematic.
**Realms in Exile (3320):** **Arnor** = "all Eriador... except the regions beyond the Lune, and the lands east of Greyflood and Loudwater" **[C, H]**; **Gondor** core Anduin-lands (Anórien/Ithilien/Anfalas... Belfalas fief) — H/M.
**Third Age:** Arnor **divided 861**: Arthedain ("between Brandywine and Lune + north of the Great Road to the Weather Hills" — **H verbatim**), Cardolan ("between Brandywine, Greyflood and the Great Road" — **H**), Rhudaur ("between Ettenmoors, Weather Hills and Misty Mountains + the Angle" — **H**); tri-point at **Amon Sûl** [C]. **Angmar** ("both sides of the Mountains", seat Carn Dûm — M) c. 1300–1975. **Gondor at its height (c. 1050)**: "north to Celebrant [→ the Field of Celebrant, per UT App. C correction] and the eaves of Mirkwood; west to Greyflood; east to the inland Sea of Rhûn; south to the River Harnen, and thence... to Umbar" — **H, the corpus's clearest imperial border**; contraction: east lost by 1856; Umbar oscillates (lost 1448 Kin-strife-aftermath, retaken 1050/lost/raided 2758...); Calenardhon ceded 2510 → **Rohan** (bounds traced in the Cirion oath — **H**: Anduin/Emyn Muil-cliff/Mouths of Entwash → Mering → mountain-wall → Isen from Adorn-fork, + Isen–Adorn tongue; **Isengard a Gondorian enclave — H**); late-Third-Age Gondor = Anórien+Ithilien(contested)+the five southern fiefs — M. **The Shire** (grant of Argeleb II, 1601: Baranduin→Far Downs; Buckland 2340; Westmarch S.R. 1452 — H). Bree-land (M pocket). **Rivendell, Lórien** (Anduin↔mountains; north to Nimrodel-falls claim — M), **Woodland Realm** (post-1050 withdrawal north of the Mountains-of-Mirkwood line — M; whole-Greenwood earlier), **Dol Guldur** march (south forest — M), **Dale+Erebor** (Celduin vales — M), **Iron Hills** (M), **Dunland** (L), **Umbar** (corsair state — M point+coast), **Harad/Rhûn/Khand** (L schematic homelands), **Mordor re-armed** (2951–3019 — H walls), **Isengard under Saruman** (2759–3019 — H ring). **Fourth Age:** the **Reunited Kingdom** (Arnor+Gondor under Elessar; Rohan confirmed by renewed Oath; the Shire a protected Free Land, Men banned; Ithilien under Faramir; **Eryn Lasgalen partitioned**: Thranduil north of the Mountains, East Lórien south of the Narrows, Beornings/Woodmen between — **H, App B verbatim**).

## REVISION 2 — the Living Map (arda_timemap_jpeg.html rebuilt)
**The untouched JPEG stays untouched**; everything is overlay. New layers over the original thirteen-slice border engine:
- **Places** — 91 sites, First Age Beleriand to Núrn, each pin visually calibrated against the map's own printed labels (three-region verification pass; ~35 corrections applied), each with story and citation; a search box flies to any of them; an **elvish-names** toggle swaps in the canon other-tongue name where one exists (Imladris, Mithlond, Azanulbizar, Orodruin…).
- **Journeys** — seven dated threads: the Ring's road Bag End→Parth Galen, Frodo & Sam to the Fire, the Three Hunters/Grey Company, Gandalf & Pippin, the Ride of the Rohirrim, the Host of the West, and There-and-Back-Again (2941, by month) — every waypoint day-stamped from App B's Great Years [C], key waypoints carrying the text's own words; a day-scrubber (Sept 22 3018 → the Morannon) animates position-markers along all threads at once.
- **⚔ Battles** — the 23 battlefields of the armies dashboard pinned in place, clicking through to their animated battle-maps.
- **📏 Measure** — two clicks give miles; scale 0.42 mi/px calibrated on Hobbiton–Rivendell ≈ 397 miles, [I]-labeled with its method.
**External information (authorized):** the interaction patterns follow the major fan cartographies, studied for this build — LotRProject (journeys-with-dates, character timelines), Arda Maps (age-sliced realms; itself Fonstad-based — Fonstad's Atlas is corpus-secondary here), ArdaCraft's Pathfinder (route quotes), 3rin.gs (dual-language labels). **No external coordinate or fact entered the data**: all positions come from the map's own printed features, all dates from App B, all place-lore from the corpus.
