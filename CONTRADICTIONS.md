# THE CONTRADICTION REGISTER — Phase 3
### Eighteen documented tensions of the corpus, classified and displayed — never resolved by the archive.
Classes: **[draft evolution]** early vs late conception · **[editorial choice vs manuscript]** published construction vs Tolkien's papers · **[unreconciled / intentional ambiguity]** the Professor left it open.

## The length of a Valian Year  
*[unreconciled / intentional ambiguity]*

Annals of Aman: 1 V.Y. = 9.582 sun-years, with Tolkien's own arithmetic; NoME's late time-schemes: 1 V.Y. = 144 sun-years.

**Authority & handling:** Never reconciled — both are the Professor's own, decades apart. The archive computes with 9.582 and flags the alternative on every pre-Sun date.

*Sources & where displayed:* HoME X (AAm) vs NoME Pt I · shown in the timeline's epoch note

## Gil-galad's father  
*[editorial choice vs manuscript]*

Earliest note (WoJ): a son of Finrod's house; published Silmarillion: son of Fingon (an editorial construction CT later regretted); latest manuscript (Shibboleth): son of Orodreth, himself moved to son of Angrod.

**Authority & handling:** The latest manuscript layer is Orodreth; the published text is acknowledged editorial. The trees draw Orodreth dashed-[M] and display all three.

*Sources & where displayed:* WoJ; Silm; HoME XII · genealogy node gilgalad

## Orodreth's own place in the House of Finarfin  
*[draft evolution]*

Published Silm: son of Finarfin, brother of Finrod; Shibboleth: son of Angrod, Finrod's nephew.

**Authority & handling:** Late manuscript (nephew) drawn, published variant noted.

*Sources & where displayed:* Silm table I vs HoME XII · genealogy

## Celeborn's origin  
*[draft evolution]*

UT main scheme: Sindar of Doriath, grandson of Elmo; a late note: a Teleri of Aman (Galadriel's kinsman there).

**Authority & handling:** UT's Sindarin descent drawn [M]; the Aman variant noted. CT himself calls the whole Galadriel-and-Celeborn history 'severely inconsistent'.

*Sources & where displayed:* UT, Hist. of Galadriel & Celeborn · genealogy

**Authority & handling:** The archive uses only movements attested per-scheme with the scheme named; no composite invented.

## The Blue Wizards  
*[draft evolution]*

The Istari essay: arrive T.A. c.1000, feared failed; Last Writings: arrive in the Second Age with Glorfindel, as 'Darkness-slayer and East-helper', undermining Sauron's rear.

**Authority & handling:** Both accounts stated wherever the Istari appear; the later note is later.

*Sources & where displayed:* UT, The Istari vs HoME XII, Last Writings · peoples/silences

## 'Noldorin' versus Sindarin  
*[draft evolution]*

The Etymologies' Beleriandic branch is 'Noldorin' (the Exiles' own tongue); the post-LotR re-conception makes Beleriand's tongue Sindarin, adopted by the Noldor.

**Authority & handling:** The languages hall files Etymologies forms under Sindarin per the later conception, with the re-basing documented.

*Sources & where displayed:* HoME V vs post-LotR texts · LANGUAGES.md §4

## The descent of all Elvish speech

**Authority & handling:** Q&E (later) structures the tree; Lhammas' scheme documented.

*Sources & where displayed:* HoME V vs HoME XI · languages tree

**Authority & handling:** UT's fuller line drawn; App A's omission flagged on the node.

*Sources & where displayed:* UT vs LotR App A · genealogy

## The origin of the Orcs  
*[unreconciled / intentional ambiguity]*

Corrupted Elves (published Silm's frame); corrupted Men, beast-forms, and Maiar-led automata are all weighed in Myths Transformed — Tolkien never settled it.

**Authority & handling:** The peoples taxonomy draws the descent-link dashed with the divergence stated.

*Sources & where displayed:* Silm 3 vs HoME X, Myths Transformed · peoples taxonomy

**Authority & handling:** The archive's chronology uses the published frame (the corpus's own narrative spine) and records the late alternative here.

*Sources & where displayed:* Silm 11 vs HoME X · timeline epoch note

## Where the éored's arithmetic bends  
*[unreconciled / intentional ambiguity]*

UT's n.36 defines the éored at not less than 120 and the Full Muster at a hundred éoreds — while Théoden's actual musters run smaller; the texts themselves show the ideal against the war-worn real.

**Authority & handling:** The armies model uses both: the definition as ceiling, the narrative musters as actuals.

*Sources & where displayed:* UT n.36 vs LotR III · ARMIES.md
