**Calendar synchronisations [all C, App D + App B]:** Shire Reckoning year 1 = T.A. 1601 (add 1600); Bree reckoning = T.A. −1299; every Shire month 30 days + lithe-days; S.R. March 25 (the Downfall of Barad-dûr) "would correspond to our March 27"; Kings'/Stewards' Reckoning in Gondor; New Reckoning begins the year on old March 25; Elvish *yén* = 144 sun-years; F.o.A. 1 is reckoned from Elrond's departure (Sept 3021), while in Gondor the New Reckoning runs from 3019 March 25. **Conflict handled:** NoME's late scheme makes 1 V.Y. = 144 sun-years; the timeline uses AAm's 9.582 [C] for display proportions and flags the alternative — both are corpus-canonical, unreconciled by Tolkien.

**Absolute axis used by the dataset/timeline:** year 0 = first sunrise (F.A. 1). Y.T. dates map to −(1500−VY)×9.582 (Awakening Y.T. 1050 = "450 of such Years before" F.A. ⇒ ≈ −4,312 [C×C]); S.A. n = 590+n; T.A. n = 4,031+n; F.o.A. n = 7,052+n. Span drawn: ≈ −14,400 … +7,180.

## 5. METHOD NOTES
Hour-level entries exist only where the text gives them ("at dawn", "the third hour", "midnight", "ere the fourth day is ended") and each is quoted in the dataset. Parallel storylines (Books III–VI) are reconciled exactly as App B does — its Tale of Years is the arbiter wherever chapter-internal reckonings drift. The two Valian-year lengths and the two Elvish-history schemes (AAm vs NoME) are shown as alternatives, not merged. Nothing undated was given a date: undatable-but-ordered events carry order-links ("after X, before Y") in the data instead of years.
