# GEOGRAPHIC RECONSTRUCTION OF THE SETTING
## (Arda / Middle-earth — reconstructed solely from the 28 documents in /home/raz/samsi)

**Method.** All 28 documents were read in full and mined for every geographic or spatial statement. The setting is a single world ("Arda") documented across three narrative ages plus a cosmological frame. Sources fall into three reliability tiers:

- **Tier 1 (canonical narrative + author-drawn maps):** (A) The Silmarillion; (B) The Hobbit; (C) The Lord of the Rings incl. Appendices and the three maps printed in it; (D) Unfinished Tales incl. its two maps; (E) Beren and Lúthien; (F) The Children of Húrin (+map); (G) The Fall of Gondolin; (H) The Fall of Númenor; (I) The Nature of Middle-earth.
- **Tier 2 (author's drafts and editor's apparatus):** History of Middle-earth I–XII (cited as HoME 1..12) — superseded drafts, but containing the author's own map scales, grids and distance computations, and the editor's (Christopher Tolkien, "CT") explicit statements of map errors.
- **Tier 3 (secondary/derived):** The Atlas of Middle-earth (Fonstad — cited "Atlas", always as interpretation, never as primary evidence); The LotR Reader's Companion ("RC" — but the many *verbatim Tolkien quotations* inside it, e.g. the unpublished Index and the 1969 Baynes-map name list, are treated as Tier-1-adjacent, cited "RC[T]"); Complete Guide; Companion & Guide; Tom Bombadil; Collected Poems.

**Precedence rule used for contradictions:** later, more specific, published statements > earlier drafts; author's text > author's map where he stated the map erred (he also said the reverse once — noted where relevant); CT's editorial testimony is decisive about what the maps actually show.

**Master scale anchors (all directly sourced):**
1. 1 league = "very nearly three of our miles"; precisely 5,000 rangar = 5,277 yd 2 ft 4 in; 1 ranga ≈ 38 in (D, Númenórean Linear Measures).
2. The Shire measures "Forty leagues... from the Far Downs to the Brandywine Bridge, and fifty from the northern moors to the marshes in the south" (C, Prologue, 2nd-edition reading; RC documents the 1st-edition "fifty" error).
3. "If Hobbiton and Rivendell are taken (as intended) to be at about the latitude of Oxford, then Minas Tirith, 600 miles south, is at about the latitude of Florence. The Mouths of the Anduin and the ancient city of Pelargir are at about the latitude of ancient Troy" (Tolkien 1967 letter, RC[T]).
4. The Misty Mountains run "from Mt. Gundabad about 200 miles n. of Rivendell, to Methedras about 400 miles s." (Tolkien's unpublished Index, RC[T]) — total chain ≈ 600 mi.
5. "It is two hundred leagues or more in straight flight from Barad-dûr to Orthanc" (C III.11); CT computed ~615 mi on the map (HoME 8) — mutually consistent.
6. LotR draft maps: squares of 2 cm = 100 miles (HoME 7, 8). The published 1955 large map "is 5 times enlarged exactly" from the 1954 general map (Tolkien, RC[T]). First-Age map: squares of 50 miles (grid A–M × 1–15, i.e. a mapped field of ~750 × 600 mi; HoME 5), rescaled by the author in 1971 to "40 miles to an inch... 50 miles to 1.25 inches = one square" (HoME 11).
7. Beleriand "was about 550 miles broad from Eglarest to the Mountains of Lune. From the Mountains of Lune to the Sea of Rhûn is, according to the LR map over 1,250 miles"; Cuiviénen ≈ "2,000 miles as the crow flies from the coast of ancient Beleriand" (I, March of the Quendi).

Confidence key: **H** = explicit in Tier 1 text or on an author-approved map, or multiply attested. **M** = single Tier-1 statement, or Tier-2 with CT's endorsement. **L** = inference, draft-only, secondary-only, or contradicted.

# 2. GEOGRAPHIC RECONSTRUCTION
*(Analytic description, as instructions to a cartographer. North-up throughout; "the corpus" = the 28 documents.)*

## 2.1 The world-frame

The setting is one world, Arda, documented in two cosmological states. **Before the Downfall of Númenor** the world is flat: a central landmass-and-sea disc (Ambar) within the transparent Walls of the World, wrapped in the Enfolding Ocean (Vaiya/Ekkaia). Its far West is the continent of **Aman**; its centre-and-east is **Middle-earth**; between them lies the Great Sea **Belegaer**, widest in the middle latitudes, pinching to the ice-strait **Helcaraxë** in the far north (the two continents' shores converge there) and interrupted southward by the **Great Gulf**. East of Middle-earth (in the Ambarkanta frame only) lies the East Sea and the Land of the Sun with its symmetric mountain-wall. **After the Downfall** the world is "bent" (made round); Aman and Tol Eressëa are removed from its circles, reachable only by the Straight Road; "new lands and new seas were made". All Third Age geography therefore sits on a round world whose north-west corner is the mapped area, at real-Earth latitudes: Hobbiton ≈ Oxford; Minas Tirith (600 mi south of it) ≈ Florence; the Anduin mouths ≈ Troy. A late astronomical reframing (Sun coeval with the world, flat-earth as legend) exists but never generated narrative geography; it is recorded, not mapped.

## 2.2 Aman (First/Second Age reference frame)

Aman's east coast faces Belegaer behind the **Pelóri**, the world's highest mountains, raised sheer after the Trees' destruction; their one pass is the **Calacirya**, roughly at the world's mid-latitude ("nigh to the girdle of Arda"). In the pass stands the green hill **Túna** with the Elf-city **Tirion** (tower Mindon Eldaliéva). Behind the wall spreads the plain of **Valinor** with **Valmar** at its heart; the Two Trees' mound stands before Valmar's *western* gate; **Taniquetil**, Manwë's seat, rises at the pass's flank "upon the margin of the sea". Mandos lies westward toward the Outer Sea; Oromë's woods and Yavanna's pastures southward (survey-point: Hyarmentir, "far south of great Taniquetil"); **Formenos** north "in the hills". Outside the wall run two coastal deserts: **Araman** (north, wide, cold, ending at the Helcaraxë mists) and **Avathar** (south, narrow, lightless, under the Pelóri's east feet south of the **Bay of Eldamar**). In the Bay, within sight of the shore, is anchored **Tol Eressëa** (haven Avallónë on its east); **Alqualondë**, the Teleri's arch-gated haven, lies on the coast "north of the Calacirya", outside the Trees' light-fan. East of Eressëa the **Shadowy Seas** and the net of the **Enchanted Isles** screen the whole coast from Middle-earth.

## 2.3 The First Age north-west: Beleriand

Beleriand is a distinct, older map that overlaps the Third Age map only at its eastern margin: the **Ered Luin** chain is common to both; Third Age **Lindon** is drowned Beleriand's surviving eastern rim (Ossiriand), and the isles **Himling** and **Tol Fuin** off the later NW coast are the unsunk tops of Himring and Taur-nu-Fuin. Alignment of the two maps therefore pins Himring due west of the later Gulf-of-Lhûn region. Scale: the author's working map ran ~750 × 600 mi at 50 mi/square; text figures below (1 league = 3 mi) fit that grid well except where flagged (§3.1).

Structure, north to south:

- **The Iron Mountains (Ered Engrin)** curve east–west across the top of the world on the edge of everlasting cold. Behind their westward bend sits **Angband**; its gate-tunnel issues *south* of the range beneath the piled triple towers of **Thangorodrim**, facing the great grass (later ash) plain of **Ard-galen/Anfauglith**, with the empty plain **Lothlann** stretching east.
- **Hithlum**, a cool rainy upland basin, hangs off the plain's west side, walled east+south by the crescent of **Ered Wethrin** and west by **Ered Lómin** near the sea; inside are Lake **Mithrim** (its own hill-chain to the west) and **Dor-lómin**. The sea reaches in at the **Firth of Drengist** through Ered Lómin (landing-place of the Noldor; Lammoth the echo-shore north of it). Southwest, over the hills that prolong Ered Wethrin "from Ivrin to Mount Taras", lies coastal **Nevrast** — a riverless hollow round the mere Linaewen, its sea-cliffs higher than its plain, with Turgon's first city **Vinyamar** under the promontory-mountain **Taras**.
- **Dorthonion**, a 60-league-wide pine highland, walls Ard-galen's south. Its north slope is gentle; its south face falls in the precipices of **Ered Gorgoroth** into the spider-haunted gutter of **Nan Dungortheb**, which runs between those cliffs and the enchanted north-march of Doriath. West of Dorthonion the **Pass of Sirion** (isle-fort Tol Sirion) is the west gate between north and south; east of it the **Pass of Aglon** (against Himring) and **Maglor's Gap** (between Gelion's two headwaters) are the east gates.
- **The March of Maedhros**: the hill **Himring** ("Ever-cold", broad, bare, flat-topped) anchors a line of heights east of Aglon; eastward again rises **Mount Rerir** (Lake Helevorn in its angle with the Blue Mountains). South of Aglon lies **Himlad** between the young rivers Aros and Celon; south-east of Celon the dark wood **Nan Elmoth**; south of it the plain of **Estolad**. A 1971 note gives exact local distances (Eöl's house 15 mi within Nan Elmoth; 65 mi NW from the wood's north point to the Ford of Aros; 45 mi NE thence to Aglon's SE corner; Aros→Esgalduin 25 mi; a northern Gelion-ford 72 mi due east of the house).
- **Doriath**, Thingol's girdled realm, occupies the wooded heart: beech-forest **Neldoreth** north of the mid-land westward bend of the **Esgalduin**, denser **Region** south of it and east to the Aros; **Menegroth**, the Thousand Caves, is cut in the rock of Esgalduin's *south* bank at the bend, entered only by its bridge; the west-march **Nivrim** crosses Sirion between the Teiglin-mouth and the **Twilight Meres** (Aelin-uial), the misty fenland where Aros meets Sirion and the great river's flood stalls.
- Mid-Beleriand drops south over the **Andram**, the "Long Wall" of hills running from Nargothrond's gorge-country east to **Ramdal**, with the lone broad hill **Amon Ereb** beyond, in far sight of Gelion. At the Andram, Sirion — having received Teiglin from the west and the Esgalduin/Aros drainage through Doriath — plunges at the **Falls of Sirion** underground for three leagues, re-issuing at the **Gates of Sirion**; south of the wall the empty tangle of **Taur-im-Duinath** fills the land between lower Sirion and Gelion.
- **West Beleriand**: between Sirion and the sea (99 leagues at widest). Under Ered Wethrin's south face lie the Pools of **Ivrin**, source of the **Narog**, which runs ~80 leagues SE-then-S to join Sirion in the willow-land **Nan-tathren**; between Sirion and Teiglin stands the forest of **Brethil** (central hill Amon Obel; the Teiglin's deep ravines — Cabed-en-Aras — cut its SW skirts just north of the Celebros inflow); between the vales, on high moors, the lone hill **Amon Rûdh** (crown 1,000+ ft). **Nargothrond**, Finrod's cave-city, opens in the *west* wall of Narog's gorge under the wooded heights of **Taur-en-Faroth**, watching the **Guarded Plain** (Talath Dirnen) north-east; the mound **Amon Ethir** stands one league east of its Doors; the battlefield **Tumhalad** lies in the angle of Ginglith and Narog to the north-west. On the coast, Círdan's walled havens **Brithombar** (R. Brithon) and **Eglarest** (R. Nenning) hold the **Falas**, with the tower **Barad Nimras** on the cape west of Eglarest; off the delta lie the **Isle of Balar** and **Bay of Balar** (Eressëa's broken "eastern horn").
- **Gondolin**: within the **Echoriath** (Encircling Mountains) east of upper Sirion — the eagle-peaks **Crissaegrim** are its southern face over the empty land **Dimbar** (between Sirion and Mindeb) — the round drained-lake plain **Tumladen** hides the city on the central hill **Amon Gwareth**. Its old entry, the Way of Escape, opened toward the Sirion gorge; the final scheme's entry is the **Dry River** bed off the **Brithiach** ford, climbing the ravine **Orfalch Echor** through the Seven Gates (Wood→Stone→Bronze→Writhen Iron at the crown→Silver→Gold→Steel). The high **Cirith Thoronath** crosses the *north* of the ring, "nighest to Angband". Iron-mine Anghabar in the ring's north.
- **East Beleriand**: 100 leagues wide from Sirion to **Gelion**, which gathers from Himring and Rerir, runs 40 leagues to its first tributary and doubles Sirion's length southward, swift and fall-less. From the **Blue Mountains (Ered Luin/Lindon)** six parallel tributaries comb west across green **Ossiriand** — Ascar (Rathlóriel), Thalos, Legolin, Brilthor, Duilwen, Adurant (the last split around **Tol Galen**) — the "Land of Seven Rivers". The **Dwarf-road** from **Nogrod** and **Belegost** (east-flank cities flanking **Mount Dolmed**) descends under Dolmed, follows the Ascar, fords Gelion at **Sarn Athrad**, and runs west over Aros toward Doriath. The old **East Road** from Taras crosses the Brithiach, skirts Doriath's north (bridge Iant Iaur over Esgalduin, Fords of Aros/Arossiach), and passes behind Himring.

Named battle-geography ties these together (First Battle east of Aros/Gelion midway and at Amon Ereb; Bragollach breaking Aglon and burning Ard-galen; Nirnaeth fought before the Sirion-passes with the mound Haudh-en-Ndengin left mid-plain; Fall of the Falas the next year). At the Age's end the War of Wrath drowned everything west of Ered Luin except the Lindon rim; the sea broke the mountain-wall southward to make the **Gulf of Lhûn**.

## 2.4 Númenor (Second Age)

A five-pointed star-isle in mid-Belegaer, nearer Aman than Middle-earth. Central disc ("Mittalmar") ~250 mi across, raised grassland; five promontories each ~100 mi wide × 200+ mi long (Forostar N, Orrostar ENE, Hyarrostar SE, Hyarnustar SW, Andustar W), making any tip-to-tip line through the centre "somewhat more than 700 miles"; area ~180,000 sq mi; the whole tilted south-and-east, so cliffs (to 2,000 ft) rim the N/W coasts and the only gentle shores splay between the two southern arms. Near the centre (slightly east) the holy mountain **Meneltarma** (~3,000 ft, spiral road S-foot→N-lip, flat hallow-summit, five root-ridges toward the five capes); the royal tomb-valley **Noirinan** opens at its southern base, source of the chief river **Siril** (south through Mittalmar, slow last 50 mi, marsh-mouths at the fisher-town **Nindamos**). **Armenelos** the royal city sits by the tomb-valley, ~40 road-mi NW of the great east port **Rómenna** at its firth (isle Tol Uinen; tower Calmindon); the one wheeled road runs Rómenna→Armenelos→Noirinan→Ondosto (Forostar)→Andúnië. The west: haven-city **Andúnië** on its northern west-bay (hill **Oromet** with Tar-Minastir's tower nearby); between Andustar and Hyarnustar the warm rain-catching **Bay of Eldanna** faces Eressëa, with the green haven **Eldalondë** at its centre, the mallorn-groves of **Nísimaldar**, and the river Nunduinë (lake Nísinen 3 mi inland). Regional economies: Forostar stone/firs (sheer sea-crag **Sorontil** at North Cape), Orrostar grain behind 2,100-ft shelter-heights, Hyarrostar ship-timber plantations, Hyarnustar vineyards, Emerië (SW Mittalmar) sheep-downs. The island drowned utterly in the Downfall; the corpus treats any surviving Meneltarma-isle as unverified legend.

## 2.5 Third Age Middle-earth, the mapped north-west

**Overall frame.** The map's spine is the **Misty Mountains**, a ~600-mi N–S chain from **Gundabad** (200 mi north of Rivendell) to **Methedras** (400 mi south of it), separating **Eriador** (west) from **Wilderland/Rhovanion** (east). The west-coast sea is Belegaer; its great inlets are (N→S) the immense ice-bay of **Forochel** (its cape's far point ~700 mi north of Carn Dûm), the **Gulf of Lhûn**, and the **Bay of Belfalas**; between Lhûn and Belfalas the coast bulges west in Minhiriath/Enedwaith with the small deep estuaries of Baranduin and Gwathló and the cape-forest **Eryn Vorn**; the SW corner of the map is the mountain-cape **Andrast**. The southern spine is the E–W **White Mountains (Ered Nimrais)**, meeting the Misty Mountains' end at the ~20-mi **Gap of Rohan** (the Isen flows through it); east of Anduin rise Mordor's right-angled fences (**Ered Lithui** N, **Ephel Dúath** W). The great river **Anduin** runs the whole map N→S between the Misty Mountains and Mirkwood, then between Rohan/Gondor and Mordor to its delta in Belfalas. East again, ~700 mi from the mountains, lies the inland **Sea of Rhûn**; south beyond the Harnen, Harad; south-east of Mordor, Khand.

**Eriador.** Between Ered Luin and the Misty Mountains, bounded south by Greyflood+Glanduin. Lindon holds the coast beyond the Lune; **Mithlond** (Grey Havens) sits on both shores of the Gulf's narrows. Inland eastward: the **Tower Hills** (Elostirion palantír, looking only to Eressëa), the **Far Downs**, the **White Downs** (Michel Delving), and the **Shire** — 40 leagues Far Downs→Brandywine Bridge, 50 leagues north moors→south marshes, quartered into Farthings around the central **Three-Farthing Stone**; **Buckland** across the Brandywine, backed by the **High Hay**, then the **Old Forest** (Withywindle valley; Bombadil's house) and the **Barrow-downs**; the **Greenway** runs north–south past their eastern skirts through the defile of **Andrath** to **Bree** at the Road-crossing (40 mi east of the Shire; villages Staddle, Combe, Archet in the Chetwood). The East Road continues past the **Forsaken Inn** (~a day east), the **Midgewater Marshes**, and the **Weather Hills** ending at **Weathertop/Amon Sûl** (~120 road-mi from Bree), then ~200 mi more, crossing the **Last Bridge** over the Hoarwell (**Mitheithel**, from the troll-fell **Ettenmoors**) through the wooded **Trollshaws**, to the **Ford of Bruinen** and hidden **Rivendell** (~20 mi beyond the Ford; ~400 mi total from Hobbiton). The Hoarwell and **Loudwater** enclose "the Angle"; united they form the **Greyflood** past ruined **Tharbad** (bridge-town of the old North–South Road; navigable from the sea, where **Lond Daer** marks the ancient haven) to the sea. North of the Shire lie the **North Downs** and ruined **Fornost** (~100 mi north of Bree), NW of them Lake **Evendim/Nenuial** with ruined **Annúminas** (source of the Baranduin); the far north-east holds **Angmar** round **Carn Dûm** at the Misty Mountains' northern head. South of Rivendell along the mountains' west flank lies holly-grown **Eregion/Hollin** (45 leagues from Rivendell to its border; ruined Ost-in-Edhil near the Moria-gate road), then the **Redhorn** group — **Caradhras**, **Celebdil**, **Fanuidhol** — over **Moria** (West-door on the Sirannon; ≥40 mi of mines; East-gate above **Mirrormere** in the **Dimrill Dale**, spring of the **Silverlode**); then **Dunland** on the west skirts at the range's far south, and through the Gap the North–South Road meets Rohan.

**Wilderland.** From the Éothéod-land at Anduin's twin sources (Greylin from Ered Mithrin, Langwell from the Misty Mountains; burg **Framsburg** at their meeting — 450 mi direct from the Limlight-inflow, 800 from Minas Tirith), Anduin runs south past the **Carrock** (Beorn's lands; the old **Old Ford**/ruined bridge of the Dwarf-road below), receives the **Gladden** amid the iris-fens of the **Gladden Fields** (~100 mi south of Rivendell's latitude), passes **Lórien** at the Silverlode/Celebrant confluence (city **Caras Galadhon**; the Naith between the rivers; Dol Guldur visible across Anduin), receives the **Limlight** out of Fangorn, bends west twice at the shoal-crossings of the **North and South Undeeps** between the **Brown Lands** (east) and the **Wold** (west), runs the rapids of **Sarn Gebir**, passes the **Argonath** into the lake **Nen Hithoel** clasped by the **Emyn Muil** (Amon Hen west, Amon Lhaw east, Tol Brandir between), and plunges over **Rauros** into the fen **Nindalf**, where the many-mouthed **Entwash** comes in from Rohan and the **Dead Marshes** spread away south-east toward the hard plain of **Dagorlad**. East of the river the great forest **Mirkwood** stretches ~from the Grey Mountains' skirts south past its Narrows to the highland of **Dol Guldur**: the Elvenking's cave-halls on the **Forest River** inside its NE edge below the **Mountains of Mirkwood**; the Old Forest Road (Great Dwarf Road) crossing its waist toward the **Iron Hills**; the man-made **East Bight** notching its east side. The Forest River and the **Running River (Celduin)** from **Erebor** fill the **Long Lake** (pile-town **Esgaroth**), which spills south over falls, the Celduin then running SE (joined by the **Carnen** from the Iron Hills) to the **Sea of Rhûn**. Erebor stands alone a day north of the Lake, ruined **Dale** in the valley between its south and east spurs, the Front Gate opening south (source of the Running River), **Ravenhill** on the southern spur; the **Withered Heath**, the dragons' breeding-ground, lies in the fork of the **Grey Mountains** to the north; **Gundabad** watches from the west at the chains' junction.

**Rohan and Gondor.** Rohan fills the grass-lands between Limlight, Anduin/Emyn Muil, Entwash-and-Mering, the White Mountains, and the Isen (holding also the Isen–Adorn tongue): Eastemnet/Wold east of the Entwash, Westemnet and the **Westfold Vale** west, **Edoras** under the mountains at Harrowdale's mouth (Snowbourn→Entwash; refuge **Dunharrow** on the shelf above; **Paths of the Dead** under the Dwimorberg to the **Morthond Vale** and the **Stone of Erech**), **Helm's Deep/Hornburg** in the Thrihyrne's coomb (15 leagues from Isengard by the author's count; ~90 mi by the maps — flagged), **Isengard** in its own mile-wide ring at the head of **Nan Curunír** by the Isen's springs, and the **Fords of Isen** (eyot-crossing) on the Road west of it. Gondor's heartland lies east and south: **Anórien** behind the beacon-line (E→W: Amon Dîn, Eilenach, Nardol, Erelas, Min-Rimmon, Calenhad, Halifirien — the last the tallest, Elendil's hallow, the computed *centre-point of the old realm*, over the boundary **Mering Stream**); the **Drúadan Forest** and hidden **Stonewain Valley** just west of **Minas Tirith**, the seven-circled city on Mindolluin's knee (Gate→Citadel 700 ft; White Tower +300 ft = 1,000 ft over the plain), ringed by the tilled **Pelennor** inside the **Rammas Echor** (10+ league circuit; 4 leagues NE to the Causeway Forts toward ruined bridge-city **Osgiliath** 12 mi away; 1 league SE where the wall overhangs the **Harlond** quays). North up the river lies the ship-isle **Cair Andros**; across it **Ithilien**, the terraced Mediterranean garden-land between river and Ephel Dúath (refuge **Henneth Annûn**; the **Cross-roads**; **Emyn Arnen** inside Anduin's knee; **Minas Morgul** in its poisoned side-valley with the low pass behind it and the **Cirith Ungol** stair-pass above); south again **South Ithilien** to the **Poros**, and the fiefs along the mountains' south flank west-to-east — Anfalas, Pinnath Gelin, Lamedon (Calembel on Ciril; Tarlang's Neck), Morthond Vale, **Dol Amroth** on its promontory in Belfalas (Elf-haven **Edhellond** near the Morthond/Ringló confluence just north), Lebennin of the five rivers, **Linhir** on the Gilrain, and the great river-haven **Pelargir** above the delta (Erech→Pelargir 93 leagues; Pelargir→Harlond 42 leagues upstream). Off the delta lies **Tolfalas**; the coast then runs south past the Harnen to the corsair-cape **Umbar**.

**Mordor.** A closed rectangle: Ephel Dúath along Ithilien, Ered Lithui along the north, meeting at **Cirith Gorgor** (gate **Morannon**, Towers of the Teeth; slag-hills and the mire-moat before it), with the double NW corner (inner ridge **Morgai**; valley-corridor behind Cirith Ungol; upland dale **Udûn** behind the Morannon closed by the **Isenmouthe**; castle **Durthang** above). Inside: the volcanic **plateau of Gorgoroth** (NW; **Mount Doom** free-standing ~40 mi from the Morgai wall and ~60 from the Isenmouthe, base 3,000 ft + cone 1,500; **Barad-dûr** on an Ered Lithui spur NE of it, joined by Sauron's league-long chasm-bridged road), the ash-plain **Lithlad** east, and the brackish inland sea **Núrnen** amid the slave-fields of **Nurn** south. Roads radiate from the Morannon (N toward Dagorlad, E along Ered Lithui — first 50 mi built, S the great Harad road via the Cross-roads over Poros and Harnen ~100 leagues toward the far sea).

## 2.6 Climates, in brief
Documented gradients: polar cold at Forochel (Morgoth's lingering cold "hardly more than a hundred leagues north of the Shire"); temperate-oceanic Shire/Bree (Oxford analogue); alpine snows on Caradhras (impassable in winter) and everlasting snow on Taniquetil, the Thrihyrne, Ered Nimrais peaks and Mindolluin; Hithlum cool and rainy (west-sea weather), Nevrast milder ("sheltered from the north"); rain-shadow steppes east of the mountains (Lothlann, the Wold, Dagorlad); Mediterranean Ithilien ("shielded from the east... open to the southern airs and the moist winds from the Sea"); man-made volcanic pall over Mordor; hot near-cloudless Harad; the Bay of Eldanna in Númenor warm and wet ("faced towards Eressëa"); the Long Winter (2758–9, five months' snow) and Fell Winter (2911, Baranduin frozen) as recorded extremes.

# 3. REMAINING UNCERTAINTIES

Each: competing interpretations → evidence → preferred reading → confidence.

**3.1 Angband↔Menegroth: 150 leagues vs ~73.** Text (A ch10; F; E) thrice states "the gates of Morgoth were but one hundred and fifty leagues distant from the bridge of Menegroth"; the author's own First-Age map, at its stated scale, yields "just under 73 leagues" (HoME 11). CT resolved it by *omitting Thangorodrim and the Iron Mountains from the published map*, leaving the northern distance visually open. Preferred: keep Menegroth and all southern Beleriand at map scale; place Thangorodrim off-map or at ~150 leagues with an expanded northern plain. Confidence in the 150-league text figure as intent: high; in any specific Thangorodrim position: low.

**3.2 Helm's Deep↔Isengard: 45 vs ~90 miles.** Text: "fifteen leagues, as the crows... make it: five from the mouth of Deeping-coomb to the Fords; and ten more... to the gates" (C III.8), corroborated by the author's timetable computations; both published maps and D's "about ninety miles in a direct line" (via the road-junction) disagree. Since Tolkien in 1965 resolved on "take the maps as 'correct' and adjust the narrative" for the Shire, but never adjusted this text, the conflict is genuine. Preferred: draw to the maps (~90 mi Hornburg→Isengard; the "15 leagues" then reads as an underestimate spoken in-story). Confidence: medium; flag on the map is warranted.

**3.3 Cuiviénen's position (two frames).** (a) Classical: a bay of the vast inland Sea of Helcar far east and north, under the Orocarni (A; HoME 10). (b) Late time-and-motion note: ~2,000 mi from the Beleriand coast, "east or southeast of the Sea of Rhûn", with the Sea of Rhûn possibly Helcar's shrunken remnant (I; CT query in HoME 11). These cannot both be drawn at one scale. Preferred for a Third-Age-based map: (b), i.e. Cuiviénen off-map to the ESE, ~750 mi beyond the Sea of Rhûn; for an Ambarkanta world-inset: (a). Confidence: medium.

**3.4 The Shire's internal arithmetic.** "A good forty miles from the Bridge to Bag End" (VI.8) and Bridge→Frogmorton 22 + Frogmorton→Bywater 18 mi cannot sit inside an E–W span of 120 mi with the Three-Farthing Stone "as near the centre... as no matter" unless the Far Downs→Bridge line is measured along a different (longer, NW–SE) axis than the Road, or "forty" absorbs ~20 mi of slack (H&S compute ~62 mi cross-country Bridge→Bag End at map scale). Preferred: draw the Shire to the published Shire-map proportions (Tolkien's chosen authority in 1965) and let the "forty miles" stand as an in-story round figure. Confidence: medium-high.

**3.5 Isenmouthe→Orodruin: 50 vs 60 miles.** Frodo forecasts "sixty miles"; Sam at the Isenmouthe judges "every step of fifty miles"; the maps went 50→80→62→50 across revisions (HoME 9). Preferred: ~55–60 mi. Confidence: medium.

**3.6 The Undeeps' definition.** D App C: the Undeeps ARE the two westward bends (Limlight entering the northern one); D Cirion note 29: the "great eastward loop" lies "between the inflow of the Limlight and the Undeeps" — implying separation. CT flags the contradiction himself. Preferred: App C (bends = Undeeps), used on the 1980 map. Confidence: medium-high.

**3.7 Nazgûl and running water.** The Hunt-papers claim all but the Witch-king feared water-crossings not "dryshod"; the author's own note calls the idea "difficult to sustain" (how did they cross Greyflood?). Not mappable; recorded as unresolved doctrine affecting route-plausibility only.

**3.8 Weathertop "half way".** In-story feel (halfway Bree→Rivendell) vs map ratio (~120:200). CT: a surviving rough estimate from drafts. Preferred: map ratio. Confidence: high.

**3.9 The Five Rivers of Lebennin.** "Lebennin of the five swift streams" is never enumerated in C. The R&B essay (I) treats Erui, Serni, Sirith, Celos, Gilrain, with Ringló draining beyond and Lefnui "'fifth'... longest and widest" but a *boundary* river. Preferred: Erui, Sirith, Serni, Celos, Gilrain as Lebennin's five; Lefnui's "fifth" name refers to the older five-rivers-of-Gondor count. Confidence: medium.

**3.10 Sammath Naur's height.** Text: door "high in the upper cone"; the 1966 Index: "cave at the base of the cone" (flagged "curiously" by RC; drawing/text conflict noted in HoME 9). Preferred: text (high on the cone). Confidence: high.

**3.11 Glanduin/Swanfleet labels.** The author's certified intent (1969 letter): Glanduin = upper course from the mountains south of Moria; Nîn-in-Eilph/Swanfleet = the *fens* of its lower course above Tharbad; the printed LotR map and first Baynes printing attach the names to the upper Isen — a pure production error, to be ignored. Confidence: high.

**3.12 Dorwinion.** Named as a wine-land reachable "up the rivers" from the Long Lake trade-web (B); no positional sentence exists anywhere in the corpus; RC confirms even the Baynes list omits it. Any placement (conventionally NW shores of the Sea of Rhûn) is map-tradition, not text. Draw only with a low-confidence marker or omit. Confidence: low.

**3.13 Cirith Thoronath north vs south.** The earliest Tale sets the Eagles' Cleft in the *southern* ring of Gondolin's mountains; every later text sets it (and the escape) in the *north*, "nighest to Angband", while eagle-eyries migrate south to the Crissaegrim. Preferred: north (final scheme). Confidence: high.

**3.14 Nargothrond's bank.** First map drew it on the east bank; corrected on the map's own western extension and in all later text (Doors in the west wall, bridge to reach them). Preferred: west. Confidence: high.

**3.15 The Great March's route.** Classical route: over Anduin and the passes of the Misty Mountains and Ered Luin (A ch3; Silvan tradition "by the pass under the Red Mountain" ≈ Caradhras, I). Late scheme (I, March of the Quendi): the entire host finds the Hithaeglir "impassible", wanders *south around* them via later-Rohan and the Gwathló, entering Beleriand from the south — in that frame the Misty and White Mountains were then continuous (no Gap). Preferred: classical for any map of the March; the late scheme recorded as a variant stratum. Confidence: medium.

**3.16 The First Age far north's extent.** QS: "a hundred leagues" between the Iron Mountains' western end and the Helcaraxë; unresolvable against the 150-league Angband problem (3.1) and never drawn. Low confidence; treat the far north as schematic.

**3.17 Rohirrim muster arithmetic.** 5,500 counted + "some three score" vs "six thousand spears" sung and Ghân's count of 6,000. Not cartographic; noted as internal rounding.

**3.18 The Éothéod message-ride.** Cirion's errand-riders "some four hundred and fifty miles to the Undeeps, and more than five hundred thence" (total ~950+) against the burg→Minas Tirith direct 800 mi: consistent only as road-distances vs crow-flight. Preferred: read both as stated (route ~15 days hard riding). Confidence: high.

**3.19 Ivrin's parent range.** "Falls of Ivrin in the southern face of Dor-lómin" (ch14) vs "at the feet of the Mountains of Shadow" (ch13): reconciled if "southern face of Dor-lómin" = the Ered Wethrin wall that bounds Dor-lómin southward. Confidence: high.

**3.20 Post-Downfall coast changes in the south.** Unique HoME 12 note: the Bay of Belfalas "much filled at the east and south", Pelargir left far inland, Tolfalas "almost destroyed". Not reflected on any published map (which post-date the change anyway). Record textually; the Third Age map already shows the resulting coast. Confidence: medium.

**3.21 Editorially-flagged map placements.** CT declares conjectural: the Great Road's course Edoras↔Fords of Isen; the precise sites of Lond Daer and Edhellond. The Drúwaith Iaur label's northern placement on the Baynes map was, per the author, "correctly placed". Confidence: as stated.

**3.22 Flat vs round world (frame conflict).** Ambarkanta/Silmarillion flat world remade round at the Downfall (canonical narrative) vs Myths Transformed/NoME round-from-the-start (late philosophical revision; "Drowning of Anadûnê" makes the Straight Road a belief). Preferred for cartography: the canonical two-state frame. Confidence: high (as the corpus's operative scheme).

# 4. CARTOGRAPHER'S SPECIFICATION

**Deliverable:** one principal sheet ("The North-West of Middle-earth at the End of the Third Age") with two insets (Númenor; Beleriand of the First Age). Optional third inset: the round-world/flat-world schema. This mirrors the corpus's own cartographic tradition and keeps incommensurable frames separate.

## 4.0 Global parameters
- **Orientation:** North at top on all sheets. (Label the Erebor vignette, if drawn, "East at top, as is usual in dwarf-maps" — otherwise ignore that convention.)
- **Scale, main sheet:** 1 unit-square = 100 miles (the author's own draft-grid). Recommended drawn scale-bar in miles AND leagues (1 league = 3 mi). Total field ≈ 1,200 mi W–E × 1,500 mi N–S: west edge just beyond the Grey Havens/Eryn Vorn coast, east edge through the eastern shore of the Sea of Rhûn, north edge through the Icebay of Forochel (with an arrow-note "northernmost coast ~700 mi N of Carn Dûm"), south edge just south of Umbar.
- **Latitude registration (annotation, small type, right margin):** Hobbiton–Rivendell line ≈ 52°N ("Oxford"); Minas Tirith ≈ 43–44°N ("Florence"), 600 mi south of Hobbiton; Mouths of Anduin/Pelargir ≈ 40°N ("Troy"). These three horizontal ticks calibrate all N–S placement.
- **Symbol set:** mountain chains in ridge-profile strokes; single peaks as triangles with name; forests as canopy-texture blocks; marsh as horizontal reed-dashes; roads as double-dashed lines; rivers as tapering solid lines; ruins as hollow squares; cities as filled squares; fortresses as keep-glyphs; battle-sites as crossed swords + date; regional names in letter-spaced capitals; political borders as dotted lines ONLY where textually attested (Rohan's grant-bounds; Gondor-at-greatest as a pale tint, optional).

# 5. FINAL MAP DESCRIPTION
*(Consolidated; accepted evidence and minimum-necessary inference only.)*

The finished work shows the north-west of a round world at the end of its Third Age, at true-Earth latitudes from about Oxford (Hobbiton) to about Troy (the Anduin mouths), on a field some 1,200 miles wide and 1,500 tall, with two insets for drowned or vanished lands of earlier ages.

On the main sheet the western third is sea and seaboard: the ice-bay of Forochel in the far north; the deep Gulf of Lhûn with the Grey Havens at its narrows; the low wooded cape of Eryn Vorn between the estuaries of Baranduin and Gwathló, where the ruined haven Lond Daer marks the old sea-gate of the south road; the long mountain-cape of Andrast; and the great Bay of Belfalas, rimmed east by Dol Amroth's promontory, the five river-mouths of Gondor's coast-fiefs, Anduin's delta with the lonely isle of Tolfalas, and, far south, the hook-harbour of Umbar. Two small islands off the north-west — Himling and Tol Fuin — are all that shows of the drowned First Age land.

Down the sheet's middle runs the 600-mile wall of the Misty Mountains from Gundabad to Methedras, pierced only at the High Pass by Rivendell, at the Redhorn Gate over Moria's forty miles of delvings, and, at its foot, by the twenty-mile Gap of Rohan. West of the wall lies Eriador: the Shire's quartered 120-by-150-mile farmland strung on the East Road; Bree at the old crossroads forty miles east; the barrow-crowned and forest-dark lands between; Weathertop's ruined watch-tower; the Trollshaws and the two rivers that meet as the Greyflood at ruined Tharbad; the emptied North Kingdom — Fornost on its downs, Annúminas by Lake Evendim — and cold Angmar under Carn Dûm at the chain's head; southward the holly-country of Eregion under Moria's three peaks, Dunland, and the wide silent moors of Minhiriath and Enedwaith.

East of the wall lies Wilderland: Anduin falling from the Éothéod's burg at its twin sources past Beorn's Carrock, the iris-marshes of the Gladden, and the golden angle-wood of Lórien; Mirkwood's two-hundred-league shadow opposite, holding the Elvenking's cave-gate on the Forest River, the old Dwarf-road through its waist, the man-cut East Bight, and the bare sorcerer's hill of Dol Guldur at its southern end; beyond the forest the Lonely Mountain with ruined Dale at its knees, the pile-built Lake-town on the Long Lake, the Grey Mountains and dragon-bred Withered Heath to the north, the Iron Hills eastward, and the rivers Running and Redwater threading south-east to the inland Sea of Rhûn at the map's edge.

The south is the land of the two kingdoms and their enemy. Rohan's grass-lands fill the square between Limlight, Anduin's western cliffs, the Mering Stream and the White Mountains, with Edoras golden-roofed at Harrowdale's mouth, the refuge of Dunharrow above it, the Hornburg in its coomb under the Thrihyrne, and Isengard's mile-wide stone ring alone in the Wizard's Vale at the head of the Isen, crossed at its eyot-fords by the great road. Eastward along the mountains' north flank the seven beacon-hills run — Halifirien, Calenhad, Min-Rimmon, Erelas, Nardol, Eilenach, Amon Dîn — to Minas Tirith, the seven-walled city on Mindolluin's out-thrust knee, its keel of rock rising seven hundred feet from gate to citadel and its white tower three hundred more; around it the tilled Pelennor within the ten-league out-wall, its river-quays at the Harlond; ruined Osgiliath spans Anduin twelve miles east, the ship-isle Cair Andros stands upstream, and across the river the garden-terraces of Ithilien climb toward the Mountains of Shadow, hiding the falls-window of Henneth Annûn, the tree-ringed Cross-roads, and dead Minas Morgul in its valley below the stair-pass of Cirith Ungol. Behind those mountains the map's black quarter: Mordor, fenced north by the Ash Mountains, cornered at the Black Gate, double-walled at the Morgai, its dale of Udûn shut by the Isenmouthe; on the inner plateau of Gorgoroth the fire-mountain Orodruin stands alone, three thousand feet of base and half as much of cone, joined by a league-long causeway-road to Barad-dûr on its northern spur; ash-plains run east, and the bitter sea of Núrnen lies amid the slave-fields of the south. South and south-east beyond the map's tinted borders lie Harad along its hundred-league road, and Khand.

The first inset shows Númenor as it was: a five-pointed star seven hundred miles from horn to horn about the holy mountain Meneltarma, its royal city and eastern haven on one road, its Elf-facing western bay green with mallorn-trees, its only soft shore along the southern strands — an island that the main map cannot show, because the sea took all of it.

The second inset shows Beleriand as it was: Hithlum's rainy basin and the echoing firth; the burned north-plain under an off-map Angband; pine-dark Dorthonion falling south in sheer terror-cliffs; hidden Gondolin in its mountain-ring and girdled Doriath about the cave-city of Menegroth; the long wall of the Andram over which Sirion dives underground; Nargothrond's gorge and the havens of the Falas; and the seven parallel rivers of Ossiriand under the Blue Mountains — the one range both maps share, so that a reader laying the insets against the main sheet sees that Lindon is Ossiriand's ghost, and the two small islands off the Third Age coast are the summits of Himring and Taur-nu-Fuin above a drowned country.
