Binding Words

Oaths, dooms, curses and prophecies — the promises the story runs on

Tolkien's plot does not turn on battles so much as on words that bind. An oath sworn in Tirion drives five hundred years of ruin; a doom pronounced at the gates of Valinor shapes every First Age tale that follows; a curse laid on one man empties a kingdom. Gathered here are the promises, dooms, curses and prophecies — what was said, by whom, upon whom, and how it fell out.

Two entries rest on texts their author later discarded and are marked abandoned rather than quietly dropped or quietly kept. And two famous sentences are not here as usually quoted: Gandalf says You cannot pass, never “shall”; and Glorfindel's prophecy (not by the hand of man shall he fall) is a different sentence from the Witch-king's own boast (No living man may hinder me). They are kept apart.
