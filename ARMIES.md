## 0. THE MILITARY CANON REGISTER

**Explicit numbers (the rails of every estimate):**
- Turgon marches from Gondolin with **"an army ten thousand strong"** (Silm 20). Nirnaeth lasts to the 5th–6th day; Húrin's rearguard of Dor-lómin dies to the last man about him.
- Rohan: a **full éored = "not less than 120 men"** and is **"one hundredth part of the Full Muster"** (>12,000 Riders); Théoden actually rides with **~6,000 spears** — "the greatest riding of the Rohirrim... since the coming of Eorl" (UT, Fords of Isen n.36). Éomer's patrol éored counts **105** (LotR III.2). Muster at Edoras: "More than a thousand" (III.6); Helm's Deep: Gamling's **"maybe a thousand fit to fight on foot"**, Erkenbrand's **"a thousand men on foot"** (III.7–8). Eorl 2510: **"some seven thousand fully-armed riders"** + hundreds of horsed archers (UT).
- Gondor 3019: the outland muster is enumerated — Lossarnach **200** ("we hoped for ten times the number"), Ringló **300**, Morthond **500 bowmen**, Ethir **~100**, Pinnath Gelin **300**, Dol Amroth **700 men-at-arms + knights** — "less than three thousands full told"; the fiefs kept most strength home against the Corsairs ("sparing only a tithe") (V.1). Aragorn sends **4,000** marching from Pelargir (V.9); the Host of the West musters **7,000** (6,000 foot + 1,000 horse), **"less than six thousands"** reach the Morannon facing **"ten times and more than ten times"** their match (V.9–10). Faramir at Osgiliath is **"ten times outnumbered"**; the Rohirrim on the Pelennor are **"thrice outnumbered by the Haradrim alone"** (V.6). Umbar: **"fifty great ships"** (dromunds) **"and smaller vessels beyond count"** (V.6/9).
- Saruman empties Isengard: **"ten thousand at the very least"** counted marching out (III.9); Gandalf: "I have about ten thousand Orcs to manage."
- Five Armies 2941: Dáin's **500** mailed Dwarves; the Elvenking's "many spearmen and bowmen"; **"three parts of the goblin warriors of the North perished"** (Hobbit XV–XVIII).
- Azanulbizar 2799: of the mustered dwarf-host **"barely half... could still stand"** (App A III).
- Dagorlad/Last Alliance: Oropher+Malgalad's Silvan host — Thranduil **"led back home barely a third"** (UT); the Alliance itself: "the hosts of Gil-galad and Elendil... recalled the glory of the Elder Days" (II.2) — **no total given**.
- Isildur's Gladden company: **200 knights and soldiers** + 3 sons, ~20 archers, ten-to-one odds (UT).
- Shire 3019: Bywater — ~**200+100** hobbits vs ~**100** ruffians; **~70 ruffians and 19 hobbits dead** (VI.8). Standing institutions: **12 Shirriffs**, the Bounders "varied in number" (Prologue).
- First Age Men: Bëor's folk field at most **2,000 grown men** (HoME XII); Bereg leads **1,000** away south (Silm 17); Haladin besieged until Caranthir breaks the siege (Silm 17).
- Nazgûl: **9**; Balrogs **"3 or at most 7"** (HoME X margin); Istari **5**.

**Formations & military art [CANON]:**
- **Thangail** "shield-fence": Dúnedain shield-wall of two serried ranks, bendable at the ends into a closed ring; **dírnaith / nernehta** "man-spearhead": wedge launched at an unarrayed enemy (UT, Gladden appendix — the corpus's only named formal drill).
- Rohirrim: the éored/éoherë cavalry system; great wedge-charges at Pelennor; shield-wall on foot in extremity (Grimbold at the Fords; Helm's Dike lines).
- Gondor: concentric fortress doctrine (Rammas → seven walls; beacon-and-errand-rider mobilization; causeway forts; river squadrons at Pelargir; Ithilien ranger companies — Henneth Annûn's 200–300).
- Angband/Mordor: mass orc infantry with troll/mûmak shock and siege trains (Grond, towers, catapults, mines; Isengard's "blasting-fire" at Helm's Deep; Uruk phalanxes with pikes vs the Deeping Wall).
- Elves: Noldorin mail-and-spear hosts by lordly houses (Gondolin's array; Fingon's banners); Sindar of Doriath rarely march beyond the Girdle; Silvan troops "ill-equipped with armour" (UT) — archery-forest fighters; Falathrim sea-craft.
- Dwarves: masked heavy infantry (Belegost's iron masks vs dragon-fire at Nirnaeth [Silm 20]); mattock-companies (Iron Hills, Hobbit XVII).
- Númenor: naval-expeditionary supremacy — Tar-Minastir's "great navy" lands at Vinyalondë and takes Sauron in the rear at the Gwathló (UT); the King's Archers' hollow-steel bows (UT/NoME).

**Women and war [CANON]:**
- Eldar: "the arts of healing... are among all the Eldar most practised by the nissi; whereas it was the elven-men who bore arms at need... Indeed in dire straits or desperate defence, the nissi fought valiantly, and there was less difference in strength and speed between elven-men and elven-women... than is seen among mortals" (HoME X, *Laws and Customs*). Model: elvish field-hosts ~100% male; last-ditch defenses (falls of cities) include fighting women — noted, unquantifiable.
- Edain: the Folk of Haleth — "their chieftainess Haleth was a renowned Amazon with a picked bodyguard of women" (UT, *Drúedain*; HoME XII) — the corpus's one institutional female unit.
- Rohan: Éowyn rides disguised — an explicit exception proving the norm ("women must ride now," Dunharrow's refuge structure).
- Dwarves: women "seldom walk abroad"; no female dwarf under arms is recorded.
- Sieges evacuate "women and children" (Helm's caves, Minas Tirith) — armies in the corpus are otherwise male. Model therefore reports **female share ≈ 0 with cited exceptions**, rather than inventing splits.

## 1. CATALOG OF WAR-RELEVANT POLITIES

### 1.1 Prose overview
**First Age, the Free:** the Noldorin exile-realms ring Angband in the Siege — **Hithlum** (Fingolfin/Fingon: the largest field army, plains cavalry on Ard-galen), **Dorthonion** (Angrod/Aegnor: highland wardens), **Tol Sirion/Minas Tirith** (Orodreth: the west pass), **Himring & the March** (Maedhros: the east; Aglon and the Gap held by Celegorm/Curufin and Maglor's riders), **Thargelion** (Caranthir), **Nargothrond** (Finrod: stealth-war doctrine after Bragollach — "no open war"), **Gondolin** (Turgon: one 10,000-strong sortie in six centuries), **Doriath** (Thingol: Girdle-defense; marchwardens under Beleg's bow; only raids beyond), the **Falas** (Círdan: walled havens + ships), **Ossiriand's Green-elves** (bowmen who refuse open war after Denethor's fall), the **Edain houses** as federate infantry (Bëor's ~2,000-man strength; Hador's greater host; Haladin stockade-fighters with the Amazon tradition), and **Belegost/Nogrod** (masked dwarf heavy-foot; the only troops who withstand dragon-fire). **First Age, the Enemy:** **Angband** — orc mass-levies bred to war, wolves and wolfriders, Balrogs (≤7), the dragon-arm from Glaurung on, and thrall-Easterling auxiliaries (Ulfang's treason at the Nirnaeth).
**Second Age:** **Númenor** — the only true blue-water military state in the corpus: royal navies, expeditionary corps, coastal forts (Vinyalondë/Lond Daer), archer corps; **Lindon** (Gil-galad: the last Noldorin kingdom's host), **Eregion** (destroyed 1697), the **Silvan realms** (Greenwood/Lórinand: numerous, lightly armed), early **Moria**; against them **Mordor** founded c. 1000 — Sauron's re-bred orc legions + Easterling/Haradrim client-kings (the Ringwraith command-caste from 2251).
**Third Age:** **Arnor→Arthedain** (dwindling Dúnedain levies; after 1975 only the **Rangers**, an itinerant warrior remnant — the Grey Company's 30 is "all... that could be gathered in haste"), **Angmar** (orc+hillmen witch-realm), **Gondor** (the model's best-documented army: feudal fief-levies + city garrison + fleet + ranger companies + beacon mobilization), **Rohan** (the éored system: a farmer-herdsman society converting ~10% of its people to disciplined cavalry), **Erebor/Iron Hills** and **Dale/Esgaroth** (axe-companies and bowmen's town-levies), **the Woodland Realm** (Thranduil's archer-host), **Lórien** (bow-and-tree defense; repels three assaults in 3019), **Isengard** (Saruman's manufactured 10,000: Uruk phalanx + Dunlending levies + wolfriders), **Mordor restored** (multi-front army groups: Morgul-host, Barad-dûr host, Núrn slave-logistics, allied Rhûn/Harad/Khand columns), **Umbar** (corsair fleet: 50 dromunds+), the **Shire** (militia-in-being: Shirriffs/Bounders, one battle in living memory), and the unorganized-but-martial: **Beornings, Woodmen, Dunlendings, Drúedain** (poisoned darts, forest denial), **Ents** (siege-breakers beyond mortal capacity), **Eagles**.

## 3. STRUCTURED DATA & THE INTERACTIVE DIAGRAM

**Dataset:** `arda_armies.json` (site + this folder) — 14 campaigns; per campaign: era-position, two sides, contingents with {people-group, n, lo, hi, conf, cite}; female-share handled as annotations (see below).

**Sex composition — modeling decision [stated, not hidden]:** every attested field army in the corpus is male, with three cited exceptions (Haleth's bodyguard of women; the nissi "in dire straits or desperate defence"; Éowyn). Painting ♂/♀ stacks would therefore fabricate percentages; instead the dashboard carries a **"Women & war" panel** quoting the three canon passages, and per-army tooltips state "recorded combatants male; exceptions noted". This follows the brief's own conservatism rule.

**Implemented:** `armies_dashboard.html` — an interactive battle-explorer:
- Timeline strip (era-segmented as in the population dashboard) with a marker per campaign; click to select.
- Main panel: two opposed **stacked bars** (Free Peoples vs Enemy) for the selected campaign; segments = contingents, colored by people-group with the same fixed palette mapping as the population dashboard (Elves 1-blue, Dwarves 2-orange, Men-of-Darkness 3-aqua, Hobbits 4-yellow, Rohirrim/Northmen 5-magenta, Gondor/Arnor/Edain 6-green, Númenor 7-violet, Orcs 8-red; creatures/others muted gray). 2px surface gaps between segments; hover tooltip per segment: number, range, confidence badge, citation.
- Overview lane: paired side-total bars across all campaigns (log-height), for cross-age comparison; toggle chips hide/show people-groups everywhere (color follows the group, never repainted).
- Confidence: segment opacity (H 1.0 / M 0.85 / L 0.55); campaigns with "insufficient data" (War of Wrath, Great Armament, Lórien assaults) appear on the timeline as hollow markers with the exact refusal sentence.
- Table view and dark mode as before.

**Canon/inference separation:** every JSON point carries `conf` and a `cite`; CANON-figure segments (Turgon's 10,000; Rohan's 6,000; Saruman's 10,000; Dáin's 500; the West's 6,000...) render at full opacity with a ★ in the tooltip.

## 4. WHAT SHAPES THESE ARMIES (synthesis)

Lifespan sets veterancy: elvish hosts never lose their veterans except to death — Gondolin's army drills for 350+ years [CANON timeline], which is why 10,000 elves anchor the Nirnaeth against ten-fold numbers; mortal armies rebuild skill every generation, which Rohan solves culturally (every herdsman rides) and Gondor institutionally (garrison + fiefs + beacons). Dwarf armies are small, slow to replace (⅓ women; late marriage) but qualitatively supreme in armor and siege-endurance — their wars are vendettas, not conquests [CANON: Azanulbizar]. Client-horde empires (Angband, Mordor, the Wainriders) trade quality for bred/tribute mass and require slave logistics [CANON: Núrn]; their hosts evaporate on command-decapitation (Morannon, Dagorlad) [CANON scenes]. Geography writes doctrine: plains → cavalry (Ard-galen patrols, the éoherë); forests → archery and denial (Doriath, Lórien, Woodland Realm, Drúedain); rivers and walls → Gondor's layered defense; the sea → Númenor's pincers and Umbar's raids.

Unnumbered by the corpus (stated, not filled): War of Wrath hosts; both Númenórean armadas; Last Alliance exact totals; Angmar's and the Wainriders' strengths; Dale/Lórien/Mirkwood 3019 numbers; all orc "populations" behind the armies; any female-combatant counts. **External information: none used.** Fonstad's Atlas (corpus-secondary) cross-checks only; where her tallies exceed canon (e.g. precise Pelennor grand totals) the model keeps its own canon-anchored ranges.
